# Technical Engineering Topics by Discipline

## Mechanical Engineering
Focuses on the design, analysis, and manufacturing of mechanical systems and processes.
- **Thermodynamics**: Study of heat, energy, and work in systems.
- **Fluid Mechanics**: Analysis of fluid behavior, including liquids and gases, in motion or at rest.
- **Mechanics of Materials**: Understanding material properties under stress and strain.
- **Robotics and Automation**: Design and control of robots and automated systems.
- **Machine Design**: Development of mechanical components and systems like gears, engines, and tools.
- **Manufacturing Processes**: Techniques such as machining, welding, and 3D printing.
- **Heat Transfer**: Study of heat conduction, convection, and radiation in systems.
- **Vibration and Dynamics**: Analysis of motion and oscillations in mechanical systems.

## Civil Engineering
Involves the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure and built environments.
- **Structural Engineering**: Design and analysis of buildings, bridges, and other structures.
- **Geotechnical Engineering**: Study of soil and rock mechanics for foundation design.
- **Transportation Engineering**: Planning and design of roads, highways, railways, and airports.
- **Water Resources Engineering**: Management of water systems, including dams, canals, and flood control.
- **Construction Management**: Oversight of construction projects, including scheduling and budgeting.
- **Environmental Engineering**: Addressing pollution control and sustainable infrastructure (overlaps with the separate Environmental Engineering discipline).
- **Urban Planning**: Designing sustainable and functional urban layouts.
- **Surveying and Geomatics**: Mapping and measuring land for construction and planning.

## Computer Engineering
Combines electrical engineering and computer science to develop computing systems.
- **Computer Architecture**: Design of processors, memory systems, and computer hardware.
- **Embedded Systems**: Development of specialized computing systems for specific tasks.
- **Operating Systems**: Design and management of software that controls hardware.
- **Networking**: Design and maintenance of computer networks and communication protocols.
- **Software Engineering**: Development of software applications and systems.
- **Cybersecurity**: Protection of systems and data from digital threats.
- **Digital Signal Processing**: Analysis and manipulation of signals for communication and multimedia.
- **VLSI Design**: Creation of very-large-scale integration circuits for microchips.

## Aerospace Engineering
Focuses on the design and development of aircraft and spacecraft.
- **Aerodynamics**: Study of air flow around aircraft and spacecraft.
- **Propulsion Systems**: Design of engines, including jet engines and rocket propulsion.
- **Flight Mechanics**: Analysis of aircraft stability, control, and performance.
- **Spacecraft Design**: Development of satellites, probes, and space vehicles.
- **Avionics**: Electronic systems for navigation and communication in aerospace.
- **Aerospace Materials**: Study of lightweight, durable materials for aerospace applications.
- **Orbital Mechanics**: Analysis of spacecraft trajectories and orbits.
- **Aeroelasticity**: Study of interactions between aerodynamic forces and structural flexibility.

## Biomedical Engineering
Applies engineering principles to healthcare and medical technologies.
- **Biomechanics**: Analysis of mechanical principles in biological systems, such as joints and tissues.
- **Medical Imaging**: Development of technologies like MRI, CT scans, and ultrasound.
- **Biomedical Devices**: Design of devices like pacemakers, prosthetics, and wearable monitors.
- **Tissue Engineering**: Creation of artificial tissues and organs for medical use.
- **Biomaterials**: Development of materials compatible with the human body.
- **Medical Robotics**: Design of robots for surgery and rehabilitation.
- **Bioinstrumentation**: Development of sensors and diagnostic tools for medical applications.
- **Regenerative Medicine**: Engineering solutions for tissue repair and regeneration.

## Environmental Engineering
Addresses environmental challenges through sustainable engineering solutions.
- **Water Treatment**: Design of systems for purifying drinking water and wastewater.
- **Air Pollution Control**: Technologies to reduce emissions and improve air quality.
- **Waste Management**: Systems for recycling, disposal, and hazardous waste treatment.
- **Sustainable Design**: Engineering eco-friendly buildings and infrastructure.
- **Environmental Impact Assessment**: Evaluating the environmental effects of projects.
- **Renewable Energy Systems**: Design of solar, wind, and other sustainable energy solutions.
- **Climate Change Mitigation**: Technologies to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
- **Hydrology**: Study of water movement, distribution, and management in the environment.
