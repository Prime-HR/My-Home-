# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 17: The Earth, Sun & Moon
### "Why day becomes night, why seasons change — the great cosmic clock"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 17 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Explain day and night. 2) Understand the moon's phases. 3) Know why we have seasons.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** The sun doesn't move across the sky — YOU do. 🌍
**Say it:** "It looks like the sun rises and crosses the sky. But the truth is stranger and grander: the sun stays still, and the Earth is spinning you through day and night. Let's read the great clock of the sky."

## SLIDE 2 — Day and night
**On-screen:** Earth spins once a day → light then dark. 🔄
**Say it:** "The Earth spins like a ball, once every 24 hours. The side facing the sun has day; the side turned away has night. As it spins, day and night sweep across the world. That spin is why the sun seems to move."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Earth's daily spin is **rotation**; its year-long trip around the sun is **revolution**."

## SLIDE 3 — The moon and its phases
**On-screen:** The moon circles Earth, reflecting sunlight. 🌙
**Say it:** "The moon doesn't make its own light — it reflects the sun's. As it circles the Earth each month, we see different amounts lit — from a thin crescent to a full moon. That's the moon's phases."

## SLIDE 4 — Why seasons change
**On-screen:** Earth is tilted as it circles the sun. ☀️
**Say it:** "The Earth is tilted, and as it travels around the sun over a year, different parts lean toward the sun. That tilt gives us our seasons — and is why some months are hotter or rainier than others."

## SLIDE 5 — It shapes life on Earth
**On-screen:** Farming, tides, time, calendars. 🌾
**Say it:** "This cosmic clock runs our lives. Farmers plant by the seasons, the moon pulls the ocean tides, and our days, months, and calendars all come from Earth, sun, and moon. Ancient Ghanaians read the sky to know the times."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Read the sky, understand your world. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: Earth's spin gives day and night; the moon reflects sunlight and shows phases; Earth's tilt and orbit give seasons; the sky runs farming and time. Challenge: watch the moon over a week and notice its shape change."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Earth spins → day/night · 2) Moon reflects light → phases · 3) Tilt → seasons.

## Quiz
