# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 37: Simple Machines & Work
### "How a small person can lift a heavy load — the science of working smarter"

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 37 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Understand what 'work' means in science. 2) Name simple machines. 3) See how they multiply force.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** How does one person lift what ten couldn't? Simple machines. ⚙️
**Say it:** "A single person can raise a heavy load, pull water from a deep well, or split a log — not by being strong, but by being smart. Simple machines multiply human force. Let's learn to work smarter, not harder."

## SLIDE 2 — Work in science
**On-screen:** Work = moving something with a force. 🏋️
**Say it:** "In science, 'work' means using a force to move something. Lifting a bucket is work; pushing a wall that doesn't move is not. Machines don't reduce the work — they make it easier to do."

## SLIDE 3 — The lever
**On-screen:** A bar and a pivot multiply force. 🪵
**Say it:** "The lever is the oldest helper — a bar resting on a pivot. Push down on the long end and a small force lifts a big load. A crowbar, a see-saw, a bottle opener, even your arm — all levers."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Machines give **mechanical advantage** — output force bigger than your input."

## SLIDE 4 — Pulleys, wheels & ramps
**On-screen:** Lift, roll, and slide with less effort. 🛞
**Say it:** "Other simple machines: a PULLEY lets you lift water from a well by pulling down; a WHEEL rolls heavy loads easily; a RAMP lets you slide a load up instead of lifting straight. Each trades more distance for less effort."

## SLIDE 5 — Machines all around Ghana
**On-screen:** Wheelbarrow, borehole, scissors, gears. 🚜
**Say it:** "These machines run daily life: the wheelbarrow on every site, the borehole pulley, scissors, gears in bicycles and engines. Spot them and you'll see human cleverness everywhere."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Work smart with simple machines. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: work means moving something with force; machines make it easier, not less; the lever, pulley, wheel, and ramp multiply force by trading distance. Challenge: find three simple machines around you and name the type of each."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Work = force × movement · 2) Levers/pulleys/wheels/ramps · 3) They give mechanical advantage.
