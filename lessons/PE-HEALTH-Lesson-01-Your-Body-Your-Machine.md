# Pillar 1 · Physical Education & Health — Lesson 1: Your Body, Your First Machine
### "Move it, feed it, mind it — it's the only one you'll ever get"

## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** You'll buy a new phone every 2 years — but you only get ONE body. 📱➡️🫀
**Say it:** "Don't scroll. You'll change your phone many times in your life. But this body? You get exactly one, for the whole journey. No replacement, no upgrade. So why do we take better care of our phones than ourselves? Let's fix that."

## SLIDE 2 — One line
**On-screen:** Your body is your first machine — move it, feed it, mind it. ⚙️
**Say it:** "Think of your body as the first and most important machine you own. Like any machine, it runs well when you move it, fuel it properly, and look after it."

## SLIDE 3 — Move every day
**On-screen:** Walk, play, dance, work — motion is medicine. 🏃🏾💃
**Say it:** "Our grandparents walked and farmed and stayed strong. Today we sit and scroll. Your body is built to move — walking, football, Azonto, skipping, even house chores. Thirty minutes of movement a day protects your heart, your weight, and your mood. Motion is free medicine."

## SLIDE 4 — Eat smart with local food
**On-screen:** Balance: energy + body-building + protect foods. 🍲
**Say it:** "You don't need foreign food to eat well. Our own foods do it all: kontomire, garden eggs, and fruits to protect you; beans, fish, eggs, and groundnuts to build you; banku, yam, and rice for energy. Balance the plate, go easy on too much sugar, oil, and soft drinks."
**🎓 Go deeper (students):** A balanced diet has **carbohydrates** (energy), **proteins** (build), and **vitamins/minerals** (protect).

## SLIDE 5 — Water & rest
**On-screen:** Clean water + good sleep = a body that works. 💧😴
**Say it:** "Two free medicines we ignore: clean water and sleep. Drink water through the day. And sleep properly — your brain and body repair themselves at night. A tired body gets sick and a tired mind makes poor decisions."

## SLIDE 6 — Mental health is health too
**On-screen:** Stress, sadness, worry are real — and normal to feel. 🧠❤️
**Say it:** "Here's something we don't talk about enough: your mind needs care like your body. Feeling stress, sadness, or worry is human, not weakness or a curse. Talk to someone you trust. Rest. And know that struggling sometimes does not mean something is wrong with you."

## SLIDE 7 — It's okay to ask for help
**On-screen:** Strong people reach out. 🤝
**Say it:** "If heavy feelings stay for a long time, or life feels too much, please talk to a trusted adult, a counsellor, a faith leader, or a health worker. Reaching out is a sign of strength, not failure. You are not alone, and support exists."

## SLIDE 8 — Why it matters
**On-screen:** No health, no dreams. Your body carries your future. 🇬🇭
**Say it:** "Every dream you have — your education, your hustle, your family — sits on top of your health. The most successful plan fails if the body and mind break down. Looking after yourself isn't selfish; it's the foundation of everything."

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** One body. Treat it like treasure. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: move daily, eat balanced local food, drink water, sleep, and care for your mind as much as your body. Challenge: today, do 30 minutes of movement and drink more water — and check on a friend's wellbeing too."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Move daily · 2) Eat balanced + water + sleep · 3) Mind your mental health, ask for help.

## Quick Quiz
1. Name the 3 parts of a balanced plate. *(Energy/carbs, body-building/protein, protect/vitamins.)*
2. Name 2 free "medicines" we often ignore. *(Water, sleep, movement.)*
3. True or false: feeling stressed or sad sometimes means something is wrong with you. *(False — it's human; talk to someone if it lasts.)*

## Teacher version (45 min)
- 0–5: "What do you do to stay healthy?" Collect honest answers.
- 5–15: Teach movement, balanced local foods, water and sleep.
- 15–28: Active break — a short exercise or dance together.
- 28–40: Gentle, respectful talk on mental wellbeing and asking for help.
- 40–45: Each sets one health habit for the week.
