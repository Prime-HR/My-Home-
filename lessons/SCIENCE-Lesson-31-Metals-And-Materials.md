# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 31: Metals & Materials
### "Ghana is rich in gold and metals — but do you know what makes them special?"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 31 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Name properties of metals. 2) Understand rust and how to prevent it. 3) Appreciate Ghana's mineral wealth.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** They once called us the Gold Coast for a reason. 🥇
**Say it:** "Ghana was named the Gold Coast because of our metal wealth — gold, bauxite, manganese, iron. But what makes metals so useful, and why does iron rust away while gold shines forever? Let's dig into the science of materials."

## SLIDE 2 — What makes a metal
**On-screen:** Strong · shiny · conduct heat & electricity · bendable. ✨
**Say it:** "Metals share useful properties: they're strong, shiny, conduct heat and electricity, and can be bent or hammered into shapes. That's why they build everything from cars to wires to roofing sheets."

## SLIDE 3 — Different metals, different jobs
**On-screen:** Iron (strong) · copper (wires) · gold (lasting). 🔧
**Say it:** "We pick metals for their strengths. Iron and steel for building because they're strong; copper for wiring because it conducts electricity well; gold for jewellery and electronics because it never rusts and lasts forever."

## SLIDE 4 — The enemy: rust
**On-screen:** Iron + air + water → rust. 🌧️
**Say it:** "Iron has a weakness: in air and water it slowly turns to rust and weakens — a chemical reaction. Rust costs the world billions, eating away roofs, cars, and bridges."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Rust is iron reacting with oxygen and water — a slow **oxidation** reaction."

## SLIDE 5 — Stopping rust
**On-screen:** Paint · oil · galvanise · keep dry. 🎨
**Say it:** "We fight rust by blocking air and water: paint and oil coat the metal, galvanising covers iron with zinc, and keeping things dry helps. Protecting metal saves money on every building and machine."

## SLIDE 6 — Ghana's mineral wealth
**On-screen:** Use it wisely — and stop galamsey. 🇬🇭
**Say it:** "Our gold, bauxite, and other minerals are a blessing — but only if mined responsibly. Galamsey poisons our rivers and land. Science-trained Ghanaians can build clean, valuable mining and manufacturing for the nation."

## SLIDE 7 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Know your metals, protect them. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: metals are strong, shiny conductors; we choose each for its strengths; iron rusts in air and water; we stop rust by coating and keeping dry; Ghana is rich in minerals to use wisely. Challenge: find one rusted metal and one protected one, and explain the difference."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Metals: strong, shiny, conduct · 2) Iron rusts (oxidation) · 3) Coat & keep dry; mine responsibly.

## Quiz
1. Name two properties of metals. *(Strong, shiny, conduct heat/electricity, bendable.)* 2. What causes iron to rust? *(Air (oxygen) and water.)* 3. Name one way to prevent rust. *(Paint, oil, galvanise, keep dry.)*
