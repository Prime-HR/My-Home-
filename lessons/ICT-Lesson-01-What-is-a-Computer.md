**Pillar:** 1 — Ghana Core Curriculum · **Subject:** 1.5 ICT
**Lesson:** 1 — What is a Computer?
**Big idea:** A computer is any machine that takes information IN, thinks, and gives a result OUT — and your phone is one.
**Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + Primary/JHS students (layered)
**Duration:** 30–45-sec social cut · 45-min class version
**By Rauf Husein · AI For Ghana · raufhusein.site**

**By the end, you will be able to:**
1. Explain what a computer is in one simple sentence.
2. Name the 3 jobs every computer does — Input, Process, Output.
3. Tell the difference between **hardware** and **software**, with a Ghanaian example.

---

## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** This phone is more powerful than the computer that landed man on the moon. 🌍📱

**Say it:** "Don't scroll. The phone in your hand right now is millions of times more powerful than the computer NASA used to land on the moon in 1969. You are carrying a supercomputer — and most people use it only for WhatsApp and TikTok. Today, let's understand what you're actually holding."

**🇬🇭 Why it stops you:** It's not about a laptop in an office. It's about the device in your pocket.

---

## SLIDE 2 — The whole lesson in one line
**On-screen:** A computer = a machine that takes info **IN**, **thinks**, and gives a result **OUT.**

**Say it:** "Forget the big definitions. A computer is simply a machine that takes in information, processes it, and gives you back a result. Type a number, get an answer. Ask a question, get a reply. That's a computer."

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** The proper words are **Input → Process → Output** — the IPO cycle behind every computer on earth.

---

## SLIDE 3 — Surprise: a "computer" is not just a laptop
**On-screen:** Phone. ATM. POS machine. MoMo. Smart TV. They are ALL computers. 🤯

**Say it:** "When we hear 'computer' we picture a laptop. But a computer is anything that follows that IN–THINK–OUT rule. Your smartphone is a computer. The ATM is a computer. The POS machine at the pharmacy is a computer. Even the MoMo system processing your cash-out is running on computers. They are everywhere."

**🇬🇭 Ghana truth:** You interact with 5–10 computers before noon and don't even notice.

---

## SLIDE 4 — The 3 jobs every computer does
**On-screen:** INPUT ➡️ PROCESS ➡️ OUTPUT

**Say it:** "Every computer does three jobs. Watch a MoMo cash-out: you type your PIN and amount — that's INPUT. The system checks your balance and confirms — that's PROCESS. It sends the cash and a confirmation SMS — that's OUTPUT. In, think, out. Every single time."

- ⌨️ **Input** — what you put in (PIN, typing, your voice, a photo)
- 🧠 **Process** — the thinking part (the "brain" works it out)
- 📤 **Output** — what comes back (the answer, the cash, the SMS)

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** The "brain" that does the processing is the **CPU**. Where it keeps information is **memory/storage**.

---

## SLIDE 5 — Hardware vs Software (body vs spirit)
**On-screen:** Hardware = the body you can touch. Software = the instructions inside. 🤝

**Say it:** "Two words you must never confuse. Hardware is the part you can touch — the phone itself, the screen, the battery, the keyboard. Software is the set of instructions that tells it what to do — WhatsApp, the MoMo app, the camera app. Hardware is the body. Software is the spirit that makes it useful. A phone with no software is just a shiny stone."

**🇬🇭 Example:** Your phone (hardware) + the TikTok app (software) = your whole online business.

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** The most important software is the **Operating System** (Android / iOS) — the boss that runs everything else.

---

## SLIDE 6 — Computers run Ghana already
**On-screen:** You can't go one day without using a computer 👇

**Say it:** "This is not foreign technology. Computers already run your daily life in Ghana."

- 💰 **MoMo & banks** — every transfer is computed and recorded
- 🏪 **POS machines** at shops and pharmacies
- 🏧 **ATMs** dispensing your cash
- 🚦 **Traffic lights** and modern cars
- 🏥 **Hospital machines** reading scans and results
- 📱 **The phone** you're watching this on

**🇬🇭 Why it matters:** The Ghanaian who *understands* these machines controls them. The one who doesn't, depends on others.

---

## SLIDE 7 — Why this is real power
**On-screen:** A computer + internet = a shop, a school, a bank, a job — from your room. 🚀

**Say it:** "Here is the part that should excite you. With one computer and internet, a young person in Tamale can run a shop that sells to Accra, learn for free, receive money from anywhere, and build a business — all from one room. No big capital. This is the great equaliser. This is why we teach it free."

**🇬🇭 The mission:** Make Ghana the AI capital of West Africa by 2030 — starting with understanding the basics.

---

## SLIDE 8 — Use it well: it only does what you tell it
**On-screen:** A computer is powerful but not wise. **Garbage in, garbage out.** ⚠️

**Say it:** "Never forget: a computer is fast, but it has no common sense. It does exactly what you tell it — nothing more. Put in wrong information, you get wrong results. And it doesn't protect you on its own — that's why you NEVER share your MoMo PIN, even if a 'computer' or a caller asks. You are the one in charge."

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** "Garbage in, garbage out" (GIGO) is a real computing principle. Good input matters.

## Quick Quiz (3 questions)
1. What are the 3 jobs every computer does? *(Input → Process → Output)*
2. Give one example of hardware and one example of software on your phone. *(Hardware: screen/battery; Software: WhatsApp/MoMo app)*
3. True or false: a computer has common sense and protects itself. *(False — it only does what you tell it; protect your PIN yourself.)*

## Teacher version (45 minutes)
- **0–5 min — Starter:** Hold up a phone. Ask: "Is this a computer? Yes or no — and why?"
- **5–15 min — Instruction:** Teach Input → Process → Output using a live MoMo cash-out example (Slide 4).
- **15–25 min — Activity:** In pairs, students list 8 "hidden computers" they use weekly (ATM, POS, TV, car…).
- **25–35 min — Sort it:** On the board, sort items into **Hardware** vs **Software**.
- **35–45 min — Share & assess:** Success = student explains a computer as In→Process→Out and gives a Ghanaian example.
