## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** Someone is trying to empty your MoMo right now. Here's their playbook. 📲💸

**Say it:** "Don't scroll — this one can save your money. Every day, Ghanaians lose their hard-earned cash to MoMo scams. Not because their phone was hacked — but because they were tricked. Today I'll show you the exact playbook these scammers use, so it can never work on you again."

**🇬🇭 Why it stops you:** Everyone here knows someone who has lost money this way.

---

## SLIDE 2 — The big secret
**On-screen:** Scammers don't hack the phone. They hack **YOU** — your trust, fear, and rush. 🧠

**Say it:** "Here's the truth the fraudsters don't want you to know. They cannot break MTN's system. So instead, they break your emotions. They make you feel rushed, scared, or excited — because a panicking person stops thinking. That's the whole trick."

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** Tricking a person instead of the machine is called **social engineering** — the #1 cause of fraud worldwide.

---

## SLIDE 3 — Trick #1: "I sent money to you by mistake"
**On-screen:** "Please madam, I sent ₵500 to your number by mistake. Reverse it." ❌

**Say it:** "The most common trick in Ghana. You get a fake SMS that looks like a MoMo credit. Then a sweet voice calls: 'Please, I sent it to your number by mistake, send it back.' But no money ever entered your wallet — the SMS was fake. If you 'send it back', you're sending YOUR real money to a thief. Always check your real balance first by dialling the MoMo code yourself."

**🇬🇭 Defence:** Never trust an SMS alone. Dial your MoMo shortcode and check your true balance before doing anything.

---

## SLIDE 4 — Trick #2: "Congratulations, you've won!"
**On-screen:** "You've won the MTN promo! Pay a small fee to claim ₵5,000." 🎉🚫

**Say it:** "The promo trick. A call or message says you've won big — a phone, a car, cash. But to 'release' it, you must first send a small fee, or share a code. Real promotions NEVER ask you to pay to receive a prize. The moment someone says 'pay small to get plenty', it is a scam. Full stop."

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** This is called **advance-fee fraud** — pay a little now for a big reward that never comes.

---

## SLIDE 5 — The deadly trap: your PIN & OTP
**On-screen:** Anyone who asks for your **PIN** or the **code in your SMS** is a thief. Period. 🔐

**Say it:** "Listen well, because this one rule stops almost every scam. MTN will NEVER call to ask for your PIN. No bank, no agent, no 'customer care' will ever ask for your secret PIN or the one-time code (OTP) that comes to your phone. That code is the key to your money. Anyone asking for it — no matter how official they sound — is a thief. You never share it. Never."

**🇬🇭 Say this out loud:** "My PIN and my SMS code are like my toothbrush — I share them with nobody."

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** **OTP** = One-Time Password. It's a second lock (**two-factor authentication**). Sharing it unlocks your account for the thief.

---

## SLIDE 6 — Trick #3: the fake link
**On-screen:** "Click here to update your account" → fake page that steals your details. 🪝

**Say it:** "Scammers send links by SMS, WhatsApp, or email: 'Your account will be blocked, click to update.' The link opens a page that looks exactly like your bank or MTN — but it's fake, built to capture your details. Rule: don't click links you didn't expect. Go to the real app or website yourself by typing the address."

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** Tricking you with a fake page to steal your details is called **phishing**. Check the web address carefully — fakes use tiny spelling changes.

---

## SLIDE 7 — Trick #4: they steal your number (SIM swap)
**On-screen:** Lose your number → lose your MoMo. 📵➡️💰

**Say it:** "A clever one: the scammer collects small pieces of your info, then convinces an agent to move your number to their SIM. Once they control your number, the OTPs come to THEM, and they raid your wallet. Protect yourself: lock your SIM with a PIN, never post your full details online, and if your line suddenly goes dead for no reason, rush to your network — it may be a SIM swap."

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** This is **SIM swap fraud** — that's why your phone number is part of your security, not just for calls.

---

## SLIDE 8 — Your 5 Shields 🛡️
**On-screen:** Memorise these. They beat 99% of scams.

**Say it:** "Five shields. Teach them to your family tonight."

1. 🔒 **Never share** your PIN or OTP — with anyone, ever.
2. 🕵🏾 **Verify before you pay** — check your real balance; call the real number yourself.
3. ⏸️ **Pause when rushed** — fear and excitement are the scammer's tools. Slow down.
4. 📱 **Lock your phone & SIM** — screen lock + SIM PIN.
5. 📢 **Report & block** — block the number and report to your network (e.g. dial your provider's help line) and the police cybercrime unit.

**🇬🇭 Golden line:** "When in doubt — don't. Stop, and ask someone you trust."

---

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + your challenge
**On-screen:** You can't be tricked once you know the trick. ✅ Protect an elder today.

**Say it:** "Recap: scammers hack people, not phones. The 'mistake transfer', the 'you've won', the fake link, the SIM swap — you now know them all. And one rule rules them all: never share your PIN or OTP. Your challenge: today, teach these 5 Shields to one parent, grandparent, or trader. They are the ones losing the most — and you can save them."

**Recap in 3:**
1. They hack **you**, not the phone (social engineering).
2. **Never share** your PIN or OTP.

## Quick Quiz (3 questions)
1. A caller says you won an MTN promo but must pay ₵50 to claim it. What do you do? *(Refuse — real prizes never require payment; it's advance-fee fraud.)*
2. "Customer care" calls and asks for the code in your SMS to "verify" you. Do you give it? *(Never — no one legit asks for your OTP/PIN.)*
3. What is the one rule that stops most MoMo fraud? *(Never share your PIN or OTP.)*

## Teacher version (45 minutes)
- **0–5 min — Starter:** Ask: "Raise your hand if you or someone you know has faced a MoMo scam." Discuss briefly.
- **5–18 min — Instruction:** Walk the 4 tricks (Slides 3–7) with role-play: one student plays the scammer, one the target.
- **18–28 min — Spot the scam:** Show 5 sample messages; class votes "scam or safe" and explains why.
- **28–38 min — The 5 Shields:** Students write the 5 Shields in their own words and one real example each.
- **38–45 min — Take it home:** Each student commits to teaching the Shields to one family member tonight; report back next class.
