# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 4: Writing That Works
### "From a job application to a business proposal — words that get results"

---

## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** The right words on paper can change your whole life. ✍️
**Say it:** "Don't scroll. A good application letter wins a job. A clear proposal wins a contract. A strong essay passes the exam. Writing isn't just for school — it's a tool that opens doors. Let's master it."

## SLIDE 2 — One line
**On-screen:** Good writing = clear thinking on paper. 🧠
**Say it:** "Writing well isn't about big words. It's about thinking clearly and putting it down simply, so the reader understands you fast."

## SLIDE 3 — Know your purpose & reader
**On-screen:** Who is reading, and what do you want? 🎯
**Say it:** "Before you write a single line, ask: who will read this, and what do I want them to do? A letter to a boss sounds different from a message to a friend. Purpose shapes every word."

## SLIDE 4 — Structure: intro, body, ending
**On-screen:** Open → make your points → close. 🧱
**Say it:** "Every good piece has three parts. An INTRO that says what it's about. A BODY with your points, one idea per paragraph. An ENDING that wraps up or asks for the next step. Structure makes you look serious."

## SLIDE 5 — Short and clear wins
**On-screen:** One idea per sentence. Cut the extra. ✂️
**Say it:** "Don't try to impress with long, twisting sentences. Say one idea per sentence. If a word isn't helping, cut it. Busy readers respect writing that respects their time."
**🎓 Go deeper (students):** Cutting unnecessary words is called **editing** — every strong writer does it."

## SLIDE 6 — Application & letter basics
**On-screen:** Address · greeting · clear request · polite close. 📄
**Say it:** "For a formal letter or application: put the address and date, a proper greeting, a clear paragraph saying who you are and what you want, and a polite close — 'Yours faithfully' and your name. Simple and correct beats fancy and confused."

## SLIDE 7 — Always edit before you send
**On-screen:** Read it twice. Fix mistakes. Then send. 🔍
**Say it:** "First drafts are never your best. Read it again — out loud helps. Fix spelling and grammar. One silly error can cost you the job. You can also ask AI to check your grammar — then keep your own voice."

## SLIDE 8 — Why it matters
**On-screen:** Your writing speaks when you're not in the room. 🚪
**Say it:** "Your letter, your application, your proposal arrives before you do and speaks for you when you're not there. Make it speak well, and you'll win opportunities you never even attend in person."

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Write clearly, win results. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: know your purpose and reader; use intro-body-ending; keep sentences short and clear; learn letter basics; always edit. Challenge: write a short application or formal letter today, edit it twice, and have someone read it."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Purpose + reader first · 2) Intro–body–ending · 3) Short, clear, edited.

## Quick Quiz
1. What two things should you decide before writing? *(Your purpose and your reader.)*
2. What are the three parts of a good piece of writing? *(Intro, body, ending.)*
3. Why edit before sending? *(To fix errors that could cost you the opportunity.)*

## Teacher version (45 min)
- 0–5: Show a messy vs clear letter — which wins?
- 5–18: Teach purpose, structure, and short clear sentences.
- 18–35: Students write a real application or formal letter.
- 35–42: Swap and edit a partner's draft kindly.
- 42–45: Final edit; keep it as a real, usable letter.
