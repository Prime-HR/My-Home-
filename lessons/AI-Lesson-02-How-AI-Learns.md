# AI For Ghana — Lesson 2: How Does AI Learn?
### "The same way you passed BECE — by studying and practising"

## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** How can a machine "learn"? The same way you passed your exams. 📖
**Say it:** "We say AI 'learns'. But a machine has no brain like ours — so how? Simple: the exact same way you passed BECE. Let me show you."

## SLIDE 2 — One line
**On-screen:** AI learns by studying millions of examples and practising — like past questions.
**Say it:** "That's it. AI studies huge piles of examples, practises again and again, and slowly gets better — just like a student grinding past questions."
**🎓 Go deeper:** This studying-from-examples is called **machine learning**.

## SLIDE 3 — The student analogy
**On-screen:** Study → attempt → check answer → correct mistake → repeat. 🔁
**Say it:** "Think of how a serious student prepares. Read the topic. Try a past question. Check the marking scheme. Fix what you got wrong. Do it again. Each round, you improve. AI does exactly this — millions of times, faster than any human."

## SLIDE 4 — AI's 'past questions' = data
**On-screen:** More practice = smarter. But the examples must be GOOD. 📊
**Say it:** "For AI, the 'past questions' are called data. The more good examples it studies, the smarter it becomes. But here's the catch — if the examples are wrong, it learns wrong things."
**🎓 Go deeper:** The examples AI studies are its **training data**.

## SLIDE 5 — Training vs using
**On-screen:** First it studies (training). Then it answers (exam day). 🎓
**Say it:** "Two stages. First, AI studies for a long time — that's training. Then it's ready to answer your questions — that's exam day. When you chat with AI, it's not studying anymore; it's using what it already learned."

## SLIDE 6 — Garbage in, garbage out
**On-screen:** Study wrong notes → fail. Same for AI. ⚠️
**Say it:** "If a student studies wrong notes, they fail the exam — no matter how hard they try. AI is the same. Train it on biased or wrong data, and it gives biased or wrong answers. The quality of the teacher decides the quality of the student."
**🎓 Go deeper:** When AI learns unfair patterns from bad data, that's called **bias**.

## SLIDE 7 — Even top students slip
**On-screen:** A brilliant student still makes mistakes. So does AI. 🤔
**Say it:** "Never treat AI as perfect. Even the best student gets some answers wrong. Always check important things yourself."

## SLIDE 8 — Why it matters for Ghana
**On-screen:** If we teach AI with Ghanaian examples, it serves Ghana. 🇬🇭
**Say it:** "Most AI today studied mostly foreign data. Imagine training AI on Twi, on our crops, our markets, our names. Then it truly understands us. That's a job for this generation — and it starts with understanding how AI learns."

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** AI learns like a student. ✅ Teach one person today.
**Say it:** "Recap: AI learns by studying examples and practising. Good examples make it smart; bad ones make it foolish. Challenge: explain to one person how AI is like a student preparing for exams."
**Recap in 3:** 1) AI learns from examples (data) · 2) Good data = good AI · 3) It still makes mistakes.

## Quick Quiz
1. How does AI "learn"? *(By studying many examples/data and practising.)*
2. Why can AI give wrong or unfair answers? *(If it was trained on bad/biased data.)*
3. What's the difference between training and using AI? *(Training = studying; using = answering with what it learned.)*

## Teacher version (45 min)
- 0–5: Ask "How did you prepare for your last big exam?" Collect answers.
- 5–18: Map each answer to how AI learns (study=data, practice=training).
- 18–30: Activity — students list what "good notes vs bad notes" means, then relate to good vs bad data.
- 30–40: Discuss bias with a simple Ghana example.
- 40–45: Each student explains AI learning in one sentence.
