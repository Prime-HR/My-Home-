# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 36: Pressure
### "Why a sharp knife cuts, a borehole pumps, and your ears pop"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 36 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Define pressure simply. 2) Understand pressure in liquids and air. 3) See pressure in everyday tools.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Why does a sharp knife cut easily but a blunt one struggle? ⚔️
**Say it:** "Same force, same hand — yet a sharp knife slices and a blunt one won't. The answer is pressure. The same idea explains boreholes, car tyres, and why your ears pop on a hill. Let's feel the science."

## SLIDE 2 — What is pressure?
**On-screen:** Force squeezed onto a small area. 👇
**Say it:** "Pressure is how much force is pushed onto an area. The same push on a tiny area gives high pressure; spread over a big area, low pressure. That's the whole idea."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Pressure = force ÷ area — small area means big pressure."

## SLIDE 3 — Why the sharp knife wins
**On-screen:** Thin edge = tiny area = high pressure. 🔪
**Say it:** "A sharp knife has a very thin edge — a tiny area — so your force becomes high pressure that cuts. A blunt edge spreads the force over more area, so it just presses. Same with a nail's sharp point."

## SLIDE 4 — Pressure in liquids
**On-screen:** Deeper water pushes harder. 🌊
**Say it:** "In water, pressure grows with depth — the deeper you go, the more it pushes. That's why a dam is built thicker at the bottom, and why divers feel it. It also pushes water up through boreholes and pipes."

## SLIDE 5 — Air pressure
**On-screen:** Air pushes on everything — even you. 🎈
**Say it:** "Air has weight and pushes on everything around us, all the time. We don't feel it because it's balanced. Changes in air pressure bring our weather, and the quick change going up a hill makes your ears pop."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Control the area, control the force. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: pressure is force on an area; small area means high pressure (sharp knife, nail); liquid pressure grows with depth; air presses on everything and drives weather. Challenge: explain why walking on soft sand is easier in flat shoes than in heels."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Pressure = force ÷ area · 2) Small area = high pressure · 3) Liquids & air exert pressure.
