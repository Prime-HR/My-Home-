# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 46: First Aid & Emergency Science
### "The few minutes before help arrives can save a life — yours or another's"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 46 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Know basic first-aid steps. 2) Handle common emergencies safely. 3) Know when and how to get help.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** When someone collapses, the first 5 minutes matter most. ⏱️
**Say it:** "Accidents happen — a burn, a cut, a fall, a child choking. Before any ambulance arrives, what YOU do in those first minutes can save a life. This is practical science everyone should know."

## SLIDE 2 — Stay calm & safe first
**On-screen:** Check danger · stay calm · call for help. 🆘
**Say it:** "First rule: don't rush into danger and become a second victim. Check the area is safe, stay calm, and call for help or send someone to. A calm helper helps best."

## SLIDE 3 — Bleeding
**On-screen:** Press firmly on the wound with clean cloth. 🩹
**Say it:** "For bleeding, press firmly on the wound with a clean cloth and keep pressing. Raise the injured part if you can. Pressure stops most bleeding while help is on the way."

## SLIDE 4 — Burns
**On-screen:** Cool with clean running water, not ice or oil. 💧
**Say it:** "For a burn, cool it under clean running water for several minutes — not with ice, toothpaste, or oil, which can make it worse. Then cover loosely with a clean cloth."

## SLIDE 5 — Choking & fainting
**On-screen:** Encourage coughing; lay a fainted person flat, legs up. 😮
**Say it:** "If someone is choking but can cough, encourage coughing. If they can't breathe, firm back blows between the shoulders can help. For fainting, lay them flat and raise the legs to bring blood to the brain."

## SLIDE 6 — Know your emergency numbers
**On-screen:** Save Ghana's emergency numbers in your phone. 📞
**Say it:** "Know how to reach help fast — save the national ambulance and emergency numbers in your phone today. Quick, clear information to responders saves precious time."

## SLIDE 7 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Be the calm one who knows what to do. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: stay safe and calm; press to stop bleeding; cool burns with water; help choking and fainting; know emergency numbers. Challenge: save the emergency numbers in your phone now, and learn one first-aid step by heart."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Safety & calm first · 2) Press bleeding, cool burns · 3) Know emergency numbers.

## Quiz
1. How do you treat a burn first? *(Cool with clean running water.)* 2. How do you slow bleeding? *(Firm pressure with a clean cloth.)* 3. What's the first thing to check at an accident? *(That it's safe — avoid becoming a second victim.)*
