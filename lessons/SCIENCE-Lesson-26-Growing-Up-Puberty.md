### "The changes everyone goes through — explained with facts, not fear or shame"

**Objectives:** 1) Understand puberty as a normal stage. 2) Know basic hygiene and self-respect. 3) Make responsible, healthy choices.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Your body is changing — and that is completely normal. 🌱
**Say it:** "Growing up brings changes that can feel confusing, and nobody talks about them clearly. Let's fix that with simple, respectful facts — so you understand your own body with confidence, not fear or shame."

## SLIDE 2 — What is puberty?
**On-screen:** The natural change from child to adult. 🔄
**Say it:** "Puberty is the natural stage, usually in the teen years, when the body matures from a child into an adult. It happens to everyone, at slightly different times. Being early or late is normal — there's no 'wrong'."
**🎓 Go deeper:** These changes are driven by natural body chemicals called **hormones**."

## SLIDE 3 — Body changes
**On-screen:** Growth, voice, skin, body shape. 📏
**Say it:** "You may notice growing taller, changes in voice, skin, and body shape, and new feelings and emotions. These are all signs your body is doing exactly what nature designed. They are healthy and expected."

## SLIDE 4 — Hygiene matters more now
**On-screen:** Wash daily · clean clothes · care for your body. 🧼
**Say it:** "As the body changes, good hygiene becomes more important — bathe daily, wear clean clothes, and care for your body respectfully. Good habits now keep you healthy and confident."

## SLIDE 5 — Emotions and respect
**On-screen:** New feelings are normal — treat yourself and others with respect. 🤝
**Say it:** "New emotions and attractions are a normal part of growing up. The key is respect — for your own body and for others. Healthy boundaries, kindness, and self-control are signs of true maturity."

## SLIDE 6 — Make responsible choices
**On-screen:** Stay in school · talk to trusted adults · protect your future. 🎯
**Say it:** "Your choices now shape your future. Focus on your education, avoid pressure to grow up too fast, and talk to a trusted parent, teacher, or health worker when you have questions. Asking is wise, not shameful."

## SLIDE 7 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Understand your body, respect yourself. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: puberty is a normal stage driven by hormones; body and emotional changes are healthy; hygiene and respect matter more; make responsible choices and ask trusted adults. Challenge: write down one question you have, and ask a trusted adult or health worker."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Puberty is normal · 2) Hygiene + respect · 3) Responsible choices; ask trusted adults.
