# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 16: Comprehension Deep Dive — Reading Between the Lines
### "The best readers understand what the writer didn't even say out loud"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 16 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Answer comprehension questions well. 2) Make inferences. 3) Find the writer's tone and purpose.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** "I'm fine," she said, looking away. Is she really fine? 🤔
**Say it:** "When someone says 'I'm fine' while looking away, you know they may not be. Great readers do this with text — they read between the lines. This skill wins comprehension marks and helps you understand people. Let's sharpen it."

## SLIDE 2 — Two kinds of answers
**On-screen:** Stated (on the line) vs implied (between lines). 📏
**Say it:** "Comprehension has two kinds of answers. Some are STATED directly — find and quote them. Others are IMPLIED — the writer hints, and you must work it out from clues. Exams test both."

## SLIDE 3 — Making inferences
**On-screen:** Use clues to reach a sensible conclusion. 🔍
**Say it:** "An inference is a smart conclusion from clues. 'He pulled his coat tight and shivered' — you infer it was cold, even though the text never said 'cold'. Look for the clues the writer left."
**🎓 Go deeper:** An **inference** is supported by evidence in the text — not a wild guess."

## SLIDE 4 — Answer in your own words
**On-screen:** Show you understood — don't just copy. ✍️
**Say it:** "When asked to explain, use your own words, as we learned in summary. Copying a sentence often loses marks; reshaping it proves understanding. Always answer exactly what's asked, fully."

## SLIDE 5 — Tone and purpose
**On-screen:** Why did the writer write this — and how do they feel? 🎭
**Say it:** "Strong readers sense tone — is the writer angry, joyful, worried? — and purpose — to inform, persuade, or entertain? Spotting these shows deep understanding and lifts your answers."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Read the lines, and between them. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: some answers are stated, some implied; make inferences from clues; answer in your own words and fully; sense tone and purpose. Challenge: read a short paragraph and write one fact it states and one thing it implies."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Stated vs implied · 2) Infer from clues · 3) Own words; sense tone & purpose.

## Quiz
1. What is an inference? *(A conclusion drawn from clues/evidence.)* 2. Why answer in your own words? *(To show you understood, not just copied.)* 3. Name one thing to sense beyond the facts. *(Tone or purpose.)*
