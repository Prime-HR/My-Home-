# Pillar 1 · Mathematics — Lesson 9: Perimeter, Area & Volume
### "How much fence, how much paint, how much water — never guess again"

**Subject:** 1.1 Mathematics · **Lesson:** 9 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Find perimeter, area, and volume. 2) Apply them to real jobs. 3) Stop wasting materials and money.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** How many blocks to fence your land? Maths knows. 🧱
**Say it:** "Fencing a plot, painting a room, filling a water tank — guess wrong and you waste serious cedis. Three measurements solve all of it: perimeter, area, and volume. Let's master them."

## SLIDE 2 — Perimeter: the distance around
**On-screen:** Add up all the sides. 📏
**Say it:** "Perimeter is the distance all the way around — add every side. For fencing a plot or buying skirting, perimeter tells you how much you need."

## SLIDE 3 — Area: the surface inside
**On-screen:** Rectangle area = length × width. ▭
**Say it:** "Area is the surface inside — length times width for a rectangle. A room 5m by 4m has an area of 20 square metres. Area tells you tiles, paint, and cloth."

## SLIDE 4 — Volume: the space it holds
**On-screen:** Box volume = length × width × height. 📦
**Say it:** "Volume is the space inside a solid — length times width times height. A tank 2m by 1m by 1m holds 2 cubic metres of water. Volume tells you capacity — water, grain, cement."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Area is in square units (m²); volume is in cubic units (m³)."

## SLIDE 5 — Real jobs
**On-screen:** Fence (perimeter) · paint (area) · tank (volume). 🏗️
**Say it:** "Match the measure to the job. Buying fence or trimming? Perimeter. Painting or tiling? Area. Filling a tank or ordering sand? Volume. The right measure saves money every time."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Measure the space, save the money. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: perimeter is the distance around; area is the surface inside; volume is the space it holds. Challenge: find the perimeter and area of a room today, and work out the paint or tiles needed."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Perimeter = add the sides · 2) Area = length × width · 3) Volume = L × W × H.

## Quiz
