# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 10: Spelling Made Easy
### "Why English spelling is tricky — and simple tricks to win at it"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 10 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Understand why spelling is hard. 2) Learn helpful patterns. 3) Use memory tricks to spell well.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Why is "through" spelt nothing like it sounds? 🤯
**Say it:** "English spelling can feel unfair — 'through', 'enough', 'knife' with a silent k. But there are patterns and tricks that make it far easier. One misspelt word on a CV can cost a job, so let's win at spelling."

## SLIDE 2 — Why it's tricky
**On-screen:** English borrowed words from many languages. 🌍
**Say it:** "English took words from many languages over centuries, each keeping its own spelling. That's why sounds and letters don't always match. It's not your fault it's confusing — but it can be tamed."

## SLIDE 3 — Sound it out — carefully
**On-screen:** Break long words into chunks. 🧩
**Say it:** "Split big words into smaller parts: 'in-de-pen-dence', 'Sep-tem-ber'. Spelling chunk by chunk is far easier than swallowing the whole word at once."

## SLIDE 4 — Learn the patterns
**On-screen:** "i before e", double letters, silent letters. 📐
**Say it:** "Helpful rules: often 'i before e' (believe, field); many words double a letter (running, beginning); some have silent letters (knee, write). Knowing the patterns removes much of the guessing."
**🎓 Go deeper:** A word's **root** stays the same: 'sign' is in 'signature' and 'signal'."

## SLIDE 5 — Memory tricks
**On-screen:** Make a phrase to remember tricky words. 🧠
**Say it:** "Invent tricks. 'Necessary' — one collar, two sleeves (one c, two s). 'Because' — Big Elephants Can Always Understand Small Elephants. Silly phrases stick in the memory."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Patterns + practice = perfect spelling. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: English borrowed widely so spelling is irregular; break words into chunks; learn patterns; and use memory tricks. Challenge: pick three words you often misspell, find a trick for each, and practise them."
**Recap in 3:** 1) It's irregular (borrowed words) · 2) Chunk + patterns · 3) Use memory tricks.

## Quiz
1. Why is English spelling irregular? *(It borrowed words from many languages.)* 2. How can long words be easier to spell? *(Break them into chunks.)* 3. Spell "because" — give a memory trick if you like. *(b-e-c-a-u-s-e.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
Spelling challenge → why it's tricky → chunking → patterns (i before e, doubles) → memory tricks → personal spelling-trick list.
