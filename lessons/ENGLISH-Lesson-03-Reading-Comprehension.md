### "Readers become leaders — here's how to read faster and understand more"

## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** Every wealthy, powerful person has one habit in common: they READ. 📚
**Say it:** "Don't scroll. Study the successful — leaders, founders, scholars — and one habit appears again and again: they read. Reading is how you put the world's best minds into your own head, cheaply. Let's learn to do it well."

## SLIDE 2 — One line
**On-screen:** Reading is thinking with someone else's mind. 🧠
**Say it:** "When you read, you borrow the thoughts of the writer. A good reader gathers knowledge faster than anyone who only watches and listens."

## SLIDE 3 — Find the main idea
**On-screen:** Ask: "What is this really about?" 🎯
**Say it:** "Don't drown in every word. As you read, keep asking one question: what is the main point here? Most paragraphs have one big idea and some supporting details. Catch the big idea first."
**🎓 Go deeper (students):** The big point is the **main idea**; the rest are **supporting details**.

## SLIDE 4 — Preview before you read
**On-screen:** Look at the title, headings, first lines. 👀
**Say it:** "Before reading fully, glance over it — title, headings, the first and last sentences. This is like checking a map before a journey. Your brain understands far more when it knows where it's going."

## SLIDE 5 — Guess hard words from around them
**On-screen:** Context gives meaning. 🔍
**Say it:** "Hit a word you don't know? Don't stop and panic. Read the rest of the sentence — often the meaning becomes clear from the words around it. This is called using context, and it's a real skill."

## SLIDE 6 — Read actively
**On-screen:** Question it · picture it · connect it. 💭
**Say it:** "Don't read like a sleepwalker. Ask questions, picture what's happening, connect it to your own life. Active reading sticks; passive reading slides off the mind."

## SLIDE 7 — Build the habit
**On-screen:** 15 minutes a day beats 3 hours once. 🔁
**Say it:** "You don't need to read a whole book today. Fifteen minutes daily — news, a story, anything that interests you — builds a powerful mind over months. And you can ask AI to summarise or explain hard passages as you go."

## SLIDE 8 — Why it matters
**On-screen:** Readers understand the world — and aren't fooled by it. 🇬🇭
**Say it:** "A strong reader passes exams more easily, understands contracts before signing, follows the news with judgement, and can't be easily deceived. Reading is quiet power that compounds for life."

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Read daily, lead eventually. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: hunt the main idea; preview before reading; guess words from context; read actively; build a 15-minute daily habit. Challenge: read something for 15 minutes today and tell one person the main idea in your own words."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Find the main idea · 2) Preview + use context · 3) 15 minutes daily.

## Quick Quiz
1. What should you always look for as you read? *(The main idea.)*
2. What does "using context" mean? *(Guessing a word's meaning from the words around it.)*
3. How long of a daily reading habit was suggested? *(15 minutes.)*

## Teacher version (45 min)
- 0–5: "What was the last thing you read for fun?" Discuss.
- 5–18: Teach main idea, previewing, and context clues.
- 18–32: Read a short passage; students find the main idea and define words from context.
- 32–42: Discuss building a daily habit.
- 42–45: Each picks what they'll read for 15 minutes today.
