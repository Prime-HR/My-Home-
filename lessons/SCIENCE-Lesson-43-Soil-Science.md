# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 43: Soil Science
### "Healthy soil is alive — and it's the foundation of every meal you eat"

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 43 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 + farmers · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Know soil types and what's in soil. 2) Understand fertility and erosion. 3) Care for soil for better farming.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** A handful of healthy soil holds more living things than there are people on Earth. 🌍
**Say it:** "We walk on it without a thought, but soil is alive — full of microbes that feed our crops. Lose your soil and you lose your farm and your food. Let's respect the ground beneath us."

## SLIDE 2 — What's in soil?
**On-screen:** Minerals + water + air + living things. 🪱
**Say it:** "Good soil is a mix: tiny rock minerals, water, air, and living things like worms and microbes that recycle nutrients. It's not just dirt — it's a living system feeding plants."

## SLIDE 3 — Soil types
**On-screen:** Sandy · clay · loam. 🏖️
**Say it:** "Sandy soil drains fast but dries out; clay holds water but can waterlog; loam — a balance — is the farmer's favourite. Knowing your soil tells you what to plant and how to manage it."

## SLIDE 4 — Fertility
**On-screen:** Nutrients feed crops; they get used up. 🌱
**Say it:** "Crops draw nutrients from soil. Farming the same land endlessly drains them, so yields drop. Adding compost, manure, and resting or rotating crops restores fertility — natural and cheap."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Planting different crops each season is **crop rotation**; it keeps soil rich."

## SLIDE 5 — Erosion: the silent thief
**On-screen:** Rain and wind wash away bare soil. 🌧️
**Say it:** "When land is left bare, rain and wind carry the precious topsoil away — erosion. It's why galamsey and deforestation are so damaging. Cover crops, trees, and terraces hold the soil in place."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Feed the soil, and it feeds you. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: soil is alive with minerals, water, air, and microbes; sandy, clay, and loam differ; fertility runs out without care; erosion steals topsoil. Challenge: feel some soil, decide its type, and name one way to keep it healthy."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Soil is alive · 2) Types: sandy/clay/loam · 3) Restore fertility, stop erosion.
