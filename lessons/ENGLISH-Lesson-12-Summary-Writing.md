# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 12: Summary Writing — Say More With Less
### "The skill of capturing a whole story in a few clear lines"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 12 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 + professionals · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Find the main points of a passage. 2) Write a short, accurate summary. 3) Cut detail without losing meaning.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Bosses and examiners love one thing: getting the point, fast. ⚡
**Say it:** "Nobody has time for long, rambling answers. The person who can say a lot in a little — a clear summary — saves everyone time and looks sharp. It's a top exam skill and a top work skill. Let's learn it."

## SLIDE 2 — What is a summary?
**On-screen:** The main ideas, in your own words, shorter. 📝
**Say it:** "A summary captures the key points of something long in a much shorter form, in your own words. Not every detail — just what truly matters. Like telling a friend the heart of a film in two lines."

## SLIDE 3 — Find the main points
**On-screen:** Ask: what is essential? What is just detail? 🎯
**Say it:** "Remember the comprehension lesson — hunt the main idea. As you read, separate the essential points from the examples and extra detail. Keep the bones, drop the decoration."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Essential = the idea collapses without it; detail = it only illustrates."

## SLIDE 4 — Use your own words
**On-screen:** Don't copy — reshape. 🔄
**Say it:** "A good summary is in YOUR words, not copied sentences. Reshaping it in your own words proves you understood it — and that's exactly what examiners reward."

## SLIDE 5 — Keep it short and accurate
**On-screen:** Shorter, but still true to the original. ✂️
**Say it:** "Cut hard, but stay faithful. Don't add your opinion or new facts. A summary should let someone who never read the original understand the main message correctly."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Less words, full meaning. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: a summary gives the main points, in your own words, much shorter, and stays accurate. Challenge: read a short news story and summarise it in two sentences for a friend."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Main points only · 2) Your own words · 3) Short but accurate.
**CTA:** @AIForGhana · raufhusein.site

## Quiz
1. What goes in a summary? *(The main points, in your own words.)* 2. Should you add your own opinion? *(No — stay faithful to the original.)* 3. What should you cut? *(Examples and extra detail.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
Read a passage → underline main points → drop detail → rewrite in own words (short) → summarise-a-news-story homework.
