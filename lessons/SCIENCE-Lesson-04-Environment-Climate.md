## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** The Accra floods and the rising heat aren't random. They're science — and a warning. 🌊🔥
**Say it:** "Don't scroll. The floods that hit Accra, the harsher heat, the unpredictable rains farmers complain about — these aren't bad luck. They're science talking to us. And the good news: if we understand it, we can act."

## SLIDE 2 — One line
**On-screen:** Everything in nature is connected. 🕸️
**Say it:** "Nature works as a connected web. Plants, animals, water, soil, and air all depend on each other. Touch one part and the rest feels it."
**🎓 Go deeper (students):** A community of living things and their environment working together is an **ecosystem**."

## SLIDE 3 — Ghana's rich environment
**On-screen:** Forests, the Volta, the coast, savannah. 🌳
**Say it:** "Ghana is blessed — forests in the south, the mighty Volta, the Gulf of Guinea coast, the northern savannah. These give us food, water, medicine, and beauty. They are wealth we must not destroy."

## SLIDE 4 — When we break the balance
**On-screen:** Galamsey, deforestation, plastic, pollution. ⚠️
**Say it:** "When we pollute rivers with galamsey, cut forests carelessly, and drown our land in plastic, we break nature's balance. Dirty water brings disease; lost trees bring floods and heat. We harm ourselves."

## SLIDE 5 — What is climate change?
**On-screen:** Extra heat trapped → weather goes wild. 🌡️
**Say it:** "Burning lots of fuel releases gases that trap extra heat around the Earth — like a blanket that's too thick. That heat shifts weather everywhere: stronger floods, longer droughts, rising seas. That's climate change, in plain words."

## SLIDE 6 — Why Ghana feels it hard
**On-screen:** Coastal cities, farming, fishing all at risk. 🇬🇭
**Say it:** "We feel it sharply. Rising seas threaten Ada and coastal communities. Unreliable rain hurts farmers. Hotter weather strains health and water. Africa pollutes the least but suffers a lot — so we must lead on solutions."

## SLIDE 7 — Solutions we can act on
**On-screen:** Plant trees · cut plastic · solar · protect water. 🌱☀️
**Say it:** "Real actions, big and small: plant and protect trees, reduce and reuse plastic, embrace solar power under our strong sun, stop galamsey poisoning our rivers, and keep our communities clean. Each choice adds up."

## SLIDE 8 — Green jobs of the future
**On-screen:** Climate work = real careers by 2050. 💼
**Say it:** "Here's the opportunity: solar installers, recyclers, climate-smart farmers, conservationists. Caring for the environment isn't only duty — it's one of the biggest job markets of your future."

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Protect nature — it's protecting you back. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: nature is a connected ecosystem; breaking its balance harms us; climate change comes from trapped heat and hits Ghana hard; but we can act and even build careers from it. Challenge: do one green act today — plant something, cut one plastic, or clean one space."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Nature is connected (ecosystem) · 2) Climate change = trapped heat, wild weather · 3) We can act — and earn from it.

## Quick Quiz
1. What is an ecosystem? *(A community of living things and their environment, all connected.)*
2. In simple terms, what causes climate change? *(Gases trapping extra heat around the Earth.)*
3. Name 2 actions to protect the environment. *(Plant trees, cut plastic, solar, stop galamsey…)*

## Teacher version (45 min)
- 0–5: "What environmental problem worries you most?" Collect.
- 5–18: Teach ecosystems and climate change simply.
- 18–30: Map local problems (floods, galamsey, plastic) and causes.
- 30–40: Brainstorm actions and green jobs.
- 40–45: Each commits to one green act this week.
