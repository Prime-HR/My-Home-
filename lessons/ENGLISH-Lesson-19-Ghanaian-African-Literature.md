# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 19: Ghanaian & African Literature
### "Our writers told the world that the African story belongs to Africans"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 19 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Know key African writers and works. 2) Understand why our literature matters. 3) Be inspired to read and write.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** For years, others told our story. Then our writers picked up the pen. ✊
**Say it:** "For a long time, books described Africa through outsiders' eyes. Then African writers said: we will tell our own story. Their words changed how the world sees us — and they came from people just like you."

## SLIDE 2 — Why our literature matters
**On-screen:** It carries our truth, history, and pride. 🇬🇭
**Say it:** "Literature is a people's mirror and memory. African writing records our history, our struggles and joys, our wisdom and humour — in our own voice. Reading it builds pride and identity."

## SLIDE 3 — Ama Ata Aidoo
**On-screen:** Ghana's literary queen — bold and proud. 👑
**Say it:** "Our own Ama Ata Aidoo wrote powerfully about African women, tradition, and change — proud, sharp, and unafraid. She showed that a Ghanaian woman's voice belongs on the world stage."

## SLIDE 4 — Other African giants
**On-screen:** Achebe · Armah · Soyinka · Adichie. 📚
**Say it:** "Chinua Achebe's 'Things Fall Apart' told the African story to the world. Ayi Kwei Armah challenged corruption. Wole Soyinka won the Nobel Prize. Today Chimamanda Adichie carries the torch. African literature is world-class."
**🎓 Go deeper:** These writers prove world-class literature can be deeply, proudly African."

## SLIDE 5 — From readers to writers
**On-screen:** The next great African author could be you. ✍️
**Say it:** "Every one of these authors began as a reader and a dreamer. With the writing and storytelling skills we've learned, plus your unique Ghanaian voice, the next great African book could be yours."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Read your story, then write the next chapter. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: African writers reclaimed our story; literature carries our truth and pride; Aidoo, Achebe, Soyinka, Adichie are giants; and you could be next. Challenge: find one book or poem by an African writer and read a few pages."
**Recap in 3:** 1) We tell our own story · 2) Aidoo, Achebe, Soyinka, Adichie · 3) The next author could be you.
**CTA:** @AIForGhana · raufhusein.site

## Quiz
1. Who is a famous Ghanaian woman writer? *(Ama Ata Aidoo.)* 2. Who wrote "Things Fall Apart"? *(Chinua Achebe.)* 3. Why does African literature matter? *(It tells our story in our own voice; builds pride/identity.)*

"Who tells our story?" → why literature matters → Ghanaian & African writers → read a short extract → students respond + pick a book to read.
