# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 9: Vocabulary Power — Grow Your Words
### "The size of your vocabulary quietly decides the size of your opportunities"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 9 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Build vocabulary actively. 2) Use context and word parts. 3) Choose precise words.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** The more words you own, the more doors open. 🔑
**Say it:** "Studies show people with bigger vocabularies earn more and lead more. Words are tools — the more you have, the more precisely you can think, speak, and persuade. Let's grow yours, the smart way."

## SLIDE 2 — Read to collect words
**On-screen:** Every page is a word harvest. 📚
**Say it:** "The richest source of new words is reading, as we learned. Read a little daily and note words you don't know. A reader naturally builds a powerful vocabulary over time without forcing it."

## SLIDE 3 — Use context clues
**On-screen:** Guess meaning from the sentence. 🔍
**Say it:** "Don't stop at every hard word. Read the whole sentence — often the meaning becomes clear from around it. This skill lets you keep reading and learn words naturally."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Word parts help too: 'tele' means far (telephone, television); 'bio' means life (biology)."

## SLIDE 4 — A word a day
**On-screen:** Learn it, write it, use it. ✍️
**Say it:** "Pick one new word a day. Write its meaning, make your own sentence, and use it in real conversation. A word isn't yours until you've used it. One a day is 365 a year."

## SLIDE 5 — Choose the precise word
**On-screen:** "Good" → great, useful, kind, tasty. 🎯
**Say it:** "Strong speakers swap vague words for precise ones. Instead of 'good' for everything, say 'delicious food', 'kind person', 'useful tool'. Precision makes you sound clear and intelligent."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** More words, more power. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: read to collect words, use context and word parts, learn one a day and use it, and choose precise words. Challenge: learn one new word today, write its meaning, and use it in a sentence with a friend."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Read to harvest words · 2) Context + word parts · 3) One a day; be precise.

## Quiz
1. What's the best natural source of new words? *(Reading.)* 2. What does 'context' help you do? *(Guess a word's meaning from around it.)* 3. Give a precise word for "good food". *(Delicious / tasty.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
Word of the day → context-clue practice → word parts (tele, bio) → swap vague for precise → start personal word journals.
