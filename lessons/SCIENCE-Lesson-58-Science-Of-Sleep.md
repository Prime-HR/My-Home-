# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 58: The Science of Sleep
### "You're not lazy when you sleep — your brain and body are hard at work"

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 58 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Understand why we sleep. 2) Know how much we need. 3) Build better sleep habits for school and health.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** The student who sleeps well often beats the one who 'studies all night'. 😴
**Say it:** "We're told that staying up all night to study is hard work. But science says the well-rested student often scores higher. Sleep isn't wasted time — it's when your brain locks in what you learned. Let's understand it."

## SLIDE 2 — Why we sleep
**On-screen:** Repair the body, refresh the mind. 🔧
**Say it:** "During sleep, your body repairs itself, your immune system strengthens, and your brain sorts and stores the day's memories. Skip it, and you're literally throwing away learning and health."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Memory moving into long-term storage during sleep is called **consolidation**."

## SLIDE 3 — Your body clock
**On-screen:** A natural rhythm of day and night. 🕐
**Say it:** "Your body has an internal clock that makes you sleepy at night and alert by day, tuned to light and darkness. Fighting it — late nights, bright screens — leaves you tired and foggy."

## SLIDE 4 — How much do you need?
**On-screen:** Teens ~8–10 hrs · adults ~7–8 hrs. ⏰
**Say it:** "Young people need about 8 to 10 hours; adults around 7 to 8. Less than that, regularly, harms memory, mood, and health. Sleep is not a luxury — it's a basic need like food and water."

## SLIDE 5 — Sleep better
**On-screen:** Routine · dark, quiet room · less screen before bed. 🌙
**Say it:** "Improve your sleep: keep a regular bedtime, make your room dark and quiet, and put down the phone before bed — its bright light tricks your brain into staying awake. Good sleep is free medicine."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Sleep well, learn well, live well. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: sleep repairs the body and stores memories; your body clock follows light; teens need 8–10 hours; routine and less screen time help. Challenge: get a full night's sleep before your next test and notice the difference."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Sleep repairs body & stores memory · 2) Follow your body clock · 3) Routine + less screen.
