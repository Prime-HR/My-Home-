# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 11: Punctuation — The Small Marks That Change Meaning
### "'Let's eat, Grandma' or 'Let's eat Grandma' — a comma saves lives"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 11 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Use full stops, commas, and question marks. 2) Use apostrophes correctly. 3) See how punctuation changes meaning.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** "Let's eat, Grandma" vs "Let's eat Grandma" 😱
**Say it:** "One little comma is the difference between inviting Grandma to eat and eating Grandma! Punctuation marks are tiny but mighty — they control meaning and make writing clear. Let's master the small marks."

## SLIDE 2 — The full stop
**On-screen:** Ends a complete thought. ⏹️
**Say it:** "The full stop (period) tells the reader 'this idea is finished — take a breath.' Without it, sentences crash into each other. End every complete thought with one."

## SLIDE 3 — The comma
**On-screen:** A short pause; separates items. ⏸️
**Say it:** "The comma gives a short pause and separates things in a list: 'I bought yam, rice, fish, and oil.' It also sets off names and extra information: 'Kofi, my friend, is here.' Used well, it makes writing flow."

## SLIDE 4 — Question & exclamation marks
**On-screen:** ? for asking · ! for strong feeling. ❓❗
**Say it:** "End a question with a question mark — 'Where are you?' Use an exclamation mark for strong feeling — 'Watch out!' But use exclamation marks sparingly, or they lose their power."

## SLIDE 5 — The apostrophe
**On-screen:** Shows ownership or missing letters. 🔤
**Say it:** "The apostrophe does two jobs. Ownership: 'Kofi's book' (the book of Kofi). Missing letters: 'do not' becomes 'don't'. Misusing it is a very common, very noticeable slip."
**🎓 Go deeper:** 'Its' (belonging) has NO apostrophe; 'it's' means 'it is'."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Small marks, big meaning. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: full stops end thoughts; commas pause and separate; question and exclamation marks show asking and feeling; apostrophes show ownership or missing letters. Challenge: punctuate this — 'where are you going kofi asked'."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Full stop ends a thought · 2) Comma pauses/separates · 3) Apostrophe = ownership or missing letters.
