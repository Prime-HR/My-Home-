# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 56: Materials of the Future
### "From plastic problems to smart materials — the science remaking our world"

**Objectives:** 1) Understand types of materials. 2) See the plastic problem and solutions. 3) Meet future materials.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** The next great fortune may come from turning waste into wonder materials. ♻️✨
**Say it:** "Materials science decides what our buildings, phones, and clothes are made of. The world is racing to replace harmful plastics with smart, clean materials. There's opportunity here for Ghana. Let's explore."

## SLIDE 2 — What are materials?
**On-screen:** Natural and made-by-humans. 🧵
**Say it:** "Materials are what things are made of. Some are natural — wood, cotton, clay, metal ore. Others are made by humans — plastics, glass, concrete. Each is chosen for its properties: strong, light, cheap, or flexible."

## SLIDE 3 — The plastic problem
**On-screen:** Cheap and useful — but it never goes away. 🛍️
**Say it:** "Plastic is light, cheap, and everywhere — but it doesn't rot, choking our gutters, beaches, and sea, as we learned. The very strength that makes it useful makes it a long-term problem."

## SLIDE 4 — Smarter solutions
**On-screen:** Biodegradable & recycled materials. 🌱
**Say it:** "Science is fighting back with biodegradable materials that break down naturally, and ways to recycle plastic into bricks, fuel, and new products. Ghanaian innovators are already turning sachet waste into building blocks."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Materials that rot away naturally are **biodegradable**."

## SLIDE 5 — Future materials
**On-screen:** Stronger, lighter, even 'smart'. 🚀
**Say it:** "The future holds amazing materials: incredibly strong yet light ones for building, materials that heal their own cracks, and 'smart' fabrics. Whoever masters materials science helps shape the world — and earns from it."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Build the future from better stuff. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: materials are natural or human-made, chosen for properties; plastic is useful but a lasting problem; biodegradable and recycled materials are solutions; future materials will be stronger and smarter. Challenge: think of one plastic item and a better material to replace it."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Materials chosen for properties · 2) Plastic = useful but lasting problem · 3) Biodegradable & recycled = the future.
