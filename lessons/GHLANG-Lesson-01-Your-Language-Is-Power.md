# Pillar 1 · Ghanaian Language — Lesson 1: Your Language Is Your Power
### "A people who lose their language lose their soul — let's keep ours"

## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** A child who can't speak their mother tongue is losing something money can't buy. 🗣️🇬🇭
**Say it:** "Don't scroll. Many young Ghanaians today can text in English but can't speak deep Twi, Ewe, Ga, or Dagbani — and they think it's a sign of being 'civilised'. It's actually a quiet loss. Let me show you the power you're carrying in your mother tongue."

## SLIDE 2 — One line
**On-screen:** Your language is your identity — keep it AND learn others. 🌍
**Say it:** "Speaking your language doesn't make you backward. It makes you rooted. The goal is both: be proud in your mother tongue and fluent in English. That's a strong African, not a confused one."

## SLIDE 3 — Proverbs: wisdom in one line
**On-screen:** "Se wo were fi na wosankofa a yenkyi." 🪶
**Say it:** "Our languages carry deep wisdom. 'Sankofa' — go back and fetch what you forgot — teaches that learning from the past is no shame. One proverb can hold a whole life lesson that a textbook can't."
**🎓 Go deeper (students):** Proverbs are compressed philosophy — short sayings holding big truths.

## SLIDE 4 — Folktales: lessons in story
**On-screen:** Anansi the spider — clever, flawed, unforgettable. 🕷️
**Say it:** "Anansi stories entertained us and taught us — about cleverness, greed, courage, and consequences. These tales are Ghana's gift to the world's storytelling. Don't let them die with our grandparents."

## SLIDE 5 — Oral tradition is real knowledge
**On-screen:** History, medicine, values — passed by mouth for centuries. 👵🏾
**Say it:** "Before books, our elders carried history, healing, names, and values in their memory and passed them down by speaking. That oral tradition is a treasure. When an elder dies untranscribed, a library burns."

## SLIDE 6 — The modern danger
**On-screen:** If it's not online, AI can't learn it. 📉
**Say it:** "Remember the Twi-and-AI lesson? If our languages aren't written, recorded, and shared online, the technology of the future won't understand us. Keeping our language alive is now also a tech mission."

## SLIDE 7 — Keep it alive — practically
**On-screen:** Speak it at home · record elders · write it online. ✍️
**Say it:** "Three simple acts. Speak your language proudly at home and with friends. Record your grandparents telling stories and proverbs. Write posts in your language online. Each act keeps a piece of Ghana alive forever."

## SLIDE 8 — Why it matters
**On-screen:** Language is the soul of a people. 🇬🇭
**Say it:** "A nation that forgets its language forgets itself. You can wear the world's clothes and use the world's tech — but speak your mother tongue, and you'll never forget who you are."

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Speak it. Record it. Pass it on. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: your language is identity and power; proverbs and folktales carry deep wisdom; keep them alive by speaking, recording, and writing. Challenge: learn one new proverb in your language today and teach it to someone, with its meaning."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Mother tongue = strength · 2) Proverbs & folktales = wisdom · 3) Speak, record, share it.

## Quick Quiz
1. What does "Sankofa" teach? *(Learn from the past; it's no shame to go back for it.)*
2. Who is the famous spider of Ghanaian folktales? *(Anansi.)*
3. Name one way to keep your language alive. *(Speak it, record elders, write it online.)*

## Teacher version (45 min)
- 0–5: Each student greets the class in their mother tongue.
- 5–18: Discuss proverbs — collect and explain 5 from the class.
- 18–32: Storytelling — a student or teacher tells an Anansi tale; draw the lesson.
- 32–42: Talk about language and technology (the AI link).
- 42–45: Each commits to one act to keep their language alive.
