# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 10: Forces & Motion
### "Why things fall, stop, and move — the rules that run every machine"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 10 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Define a force. 2) Understand gravity and friction. 3) See forces in machines and daily life.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Why does a dropped ball fall but never float up? ⚽
**Say it:** "Throw something up — it always comes down. A trotro needs brakes to stop. A nail grips wood. Invisible forces are pushing and pulling everything, all the time. Let's see the rules."

## SLIDE 2 — What is a force?
**On-screen:** A push or a pull. 👐
**Say it:** "A force is simply a push or a pull. It can start something moving, stop it, speed it up, slow it down, or change its direction. Every motion you see is forces at work."

## SLIDE 3 — Gravity
**On-screen:** The pull that holds us to the Earth. 🌍
**Say it:** "Gravity is the force pulling everything toward the Earth. It's why things fall, why water flows down, and why we don't float away. The same force keeps the moon circling us."

## SLIDE 4 — Friction
**On-screen:** The force that resists sliding. 🛑
**Say it:** "Friction is the force between surfaces that resists movement. It lets you walk without slipping and lets brakes stop a car — but it also wears things out and slows machines. Useful and costly at once."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Less friction = smoother/faster (oil); more friction = better grip (tyres)."

## SLIDE 5 — Forces in machines
**On-screen:** Levers, pulleys, wheels multiply your force. ⚙️
**Say it:** "Simple machines — levers, pulleys, wheels, ramps — let a small force do a big job. A wheelbarrow, a bottle opener, a borehole pulley: all multiply human strength using force science."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Master forces, master machines. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: a force is a push or pull; gravity pulls us down; friction resists sliding (helpful and harmful); machines multiply force. Challenge: find one example each of gravity, friction, and a simple machine around you."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Force = push/pull · 2) Gravity pulls down, friction resists · 3) Machines multiply force.

## Quiz
1. What is a force? *(A push or a pull.)* 2. What force pulls things to Earth? *(Gravity.)* 3. What force helps brakes stop a car? *(Friction.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
Drop and slide objects → teach force, gravity, friction → demo a simple machine (lever) → find forces around school → homework hunt.
