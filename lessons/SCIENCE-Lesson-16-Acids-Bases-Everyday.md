# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 16: Acids, Bases & Everyday Chemistry
### "From soap to lime to your own stomach — the chemistry you use daily"

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 16 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Tell acids from bases. 2) Recognise them in everyday life. 3) Understand neutralisation.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Lime is sour, soap is slippery — that's chemistry you can taste and feel. 🍋
**Say it:** "Why is lime sour and soap slippery? Why does ash help clean and lime relieve a sting? You use acids and bases every day without knowing. Let's name the chemistry in your kitchen and bathroom."

## SLIDE 2 — Acids
**On-screen:** Sour taste — lime, vinegar, your stomach. 🍋
**Say it:** "Acids tend to taste sour — like lime, vinegar, and the juice in tomatoes. Your own stomach uses a strong acid to digest food. Strong acids can be dangerous, so we respect them."

## SLIDE 3 — Bases (alkalis)
**On-screen:** Slippery, bitter — soap, ash, baking soda. 🧼
**Say it:** "Bases feel slippery and taste bitter — soap, wood ash, and baking soda are bases. That slippery feel of soap is the base reacting with oils to clean."
**🎓 Go deeper:** We measure acidity on the **pH scale**: below 7 acidic, 7 neutral, above 7 basic."

## SLIDE 4 — Neutralisation
**On-screen:** Acid + base cancel each other out. ⚖️
**Say it:** "When you mix an acid and a base, they cancel out — neutralisation. This is why an antacid relieves a sour stomach, and why lime can soothe certain stings. Opposites balance."

## SLIDE 5 — Everyday uses
**On-screen:** Cleaning, cooking, farming, health. 🌾
**Say it:** "This chemistry is practical. Soap (base) cleans grease; lime treats acidic soil for farming; baking soda (base) makes bread rise; antacids settle the stomach. Knowing acid from base helps you use them wisely."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Taste, touch, and understand chemistry. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: acids are sour (lime, vinegar); bases are slippery and bitter (soap, ash); the pH scale measures them; acid plus base neutralise. Challenge: name one acid and one base in your home and what each is used for."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Acids = sour · 2) Bases = slippery/bitter · 3) Acid + base = neutralise.

## Quiz
1. Name one common acid. *(Lime, vinegar, stomach acid.)* 2. Name one common base. *(Soap, ash, baking soda.)* 3. What happens when acid meets base? *(They neutralise/cancel out.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
Taste-safe sour vs feel soap → teach acids and bases → pH scale → safe neutralisation demo (baking soda + vinegar) → home acid/base hunt.

*Safety note: never taste or mix unknown chemicals; use only safe, known kitchen items.*
