# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 13: Essay Writing — Structure & Argument
### "A great essay isn't about big words — it's about clear, ordered thinking"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 13 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Structure an essay. 2) Build a clear argument. 3) Write strong intros and conclusions.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Examiners can tell in 30 seconds if your essay will pass. 📄
**Say it:** "An examiner reading hundreds of scripts decides fast. A well-structured essay grabs marks; a rambling one loses them — even with good ideas. Structure is the secret. Let's build essays that win."

## SLIDE 2 — The basic shape
**On-screen:** Introduction · Body · Conclusion. 🧱
**Say it:** "Every good essay has three parts: an INTRODUCTION that says what you'll argue, a BODY of paragraphs each making one point, and a CONCLUSION that sums up. Simple, powerful, expected."

## SLIDE 3 — The introduction
**On-screen:** State your topic and your stance. 🎯
**Say it:** "Open by telling the reader your topic and your position. Don't keep them guessing. 'In this essay I will argue that...' A clear introduction promises a clear journey."

## SLIDE 4 — One idea per paragraph
**On-screen:** Point → explain → example. 🔗
**Say it:** "In the body, give each paragraph one main idea. State the point, explain it, and back it with an example or reason. New idea, new paragraph. This order makes your thinking easy to follow."
**🎓 Go deeper:** A strong paragraph: Point, Explain, Example (PEE)."

## SLIDE 5 — The conclusion
**On-screen:** Restate, don't introduce new ideas. ✅
**Say it:** "End by summing up your main points and restating your position. Don't add new arguments at the end. A good conclusion leaves the reader sure of what you said."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Structure carries your ideas. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: essays have an introduction, body, and conclusion; state your stance early; one idea per paragraph with point-explain-example; sum up at the end. Challenge: plan an essay on 'Should phones be allowed in school?' with three body points."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Intro–body–conclusion · 2) One idea per paragraph (PEE) · 3) Conclude, don't add new points.

## Quiz
1. Name the three parts of an essay. *(Introduction, body, conclusion.)* 2. How many main ideas per paragraph? *(One.)* 3. What should you NOT do in a conclusion? *(Introduce new ideas.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
Show a strong vs weak essay → teach the structure → plan a topic together (3 points) → PEE paragraphs → students draft an intro + one body paragraph.
