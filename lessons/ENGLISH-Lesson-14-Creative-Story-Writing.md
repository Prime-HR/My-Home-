# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 14: Creative & Story Writing
### "Ghana is a land of storytellers — learn to put yours on the page"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 14 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Understand story structure. 2) Create characters and conflict. 3) Write a vivid short story.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** From Anansi by the fire to bestselling books — it starts with a story. 📖
**Say it:** "Ghana has told stories for centuries — Anansi tales under the moon. Today those storytellers become authors, scriptwriters, and content creators. Your imagination is a treasure. Let's learn to write it down powerfully."

## SLIDE 2 — Every story has a shape
**On-screen:** Beginning · Middle · End. 🎬
**Say it:** "A story has a beginning that sets the scene, a middle where trouble happens, and an end that resolves it. Even a 30-second TikTok follows this. Shape gives your story power."

## SLIDE 3 — Characters we care about
**On-screen:** Give them a want and a flaw. 👤
**Say it:** "Readers follow characters they care about. Give your character something they WANT and a problem in the way. A market girl who dreams of school but has no fees — instantly we care."
**🎓 Go deeper:** The problem driving the story is the **conflict** — no conflict, no story."

## SLIDE 4 — Show, don't just tell
**On-screen:** "Her hands shook" beats "She was afraid". 🎨
**Say it:** "Don't just say 'she was afraid' — show it: 'her hands shook and her voice fell to a whisper.' Showing puts the reader inside the moment. Use the senses — what they saw, heard, smelt."

## SLIDE 5 — Use Ghanaian colour
**On-screen:** Real places, names, foods, life. 🇬🇭
**Say it:** "Your stories are richest when they're truly yours — set them in Kejetia or a village, with real names, foods, and rhythms of Ghanaian life. The world is hungry for authentic African stories. Yours matters."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Tell your story — the world is listening. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: stories need a beginning, middle, and end; characters need a want and a problem; show don't tell; and Ghanaian colour makes them shine. Challenge: write a short story in five sentences about someone chasing a dream."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Beginning–middle–end · 2) Character + want + conflict · 3) Show, don't tell.
**CTA:** @AIForGhana · raufhusein.site

## Quiz
1. Name the three parts of a story. *(Beginning, middle, end.)* 2. What drives a story? *(Conflict / a problem.)* 3. What does "show, don't tell" mean? *(Reveal feelings through actions/senses, not flat statements.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
Tell a quick Anansi tale → story shape → create a character (want + problem) → show-don't-tell practice → write a 5-sentence story.
