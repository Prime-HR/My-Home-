# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 57: The Chemistry of Cooking
### "Your kitchen is a chemistry lab — and you're already the scientist"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 57 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) See cooking as chemistry. 2) Understand heat, fermentation, and mixing in food. 3) Cook safer and smarter.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Every time you cook, you run real chemical experiments. 🍳
**Say it:** "Why does dough rise? Why does meat brown and smell so good? Why does kenkey sour? Your kitchen is one of the best science labs in Ghana — and you've been the scientist all along. Let's understand the magic."

## SLIDE 2 — Heat changes food
**On-screen:** Cooking transforms taste, texture, safety. 🔥
**Say it:** "Heat doesn't just warm food — it changes it chemically. It makes raw food digestible, kills germs, and creates new flavours and smells. That delicious brown on grilled meat is a chemical reaction."
**🎓 Go deeper:** The tasty browning of food is the **Maillard reaction**."

## SLIDE 3 — Fermentation flavours
**On-screen:** Microbes make banku, kenkey, pito. 🦠
**Say it:** "Remember the microbes lesson — friendly microbes ferment our foods, giving banku and kenkey their special sour taste and helping preserve them. Our traditional foods are advanced food science passed down for generations."

## SLIDE 4 — Mixing and reactions
**On-screen:** Baking soda rises; lime curdles milk. 🧁
**Say it:** "Baking soda releases gas that makes cakes and bread rise. Acid like lime can curdle milk. These are acid-base and gas reactions from our chemistry lessons — happening right in your bowl."

## SLIDE 5 — Cook safely & keep nutrients
**On-screen:** Cook through · don't overcook · clean hands. 🧼
**Say it:** "Good food science protects you: cook meat and fish through to kill germs, but don't overcook vegetables to keep their vitamins, and always handle food with clean hands. Tasty AND healthy is the goal."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Cook with science, eat with pride. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: cooking is chemistry; heat transforms and protects food; microbes ferment our dishes; baking soda and acids cause reactions; cook safely and keep nutrients. Challenge: watch one chemical change as you cook — rising, browning, or souring — and name it."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Cooking = chemistry · 2) Heat + microbes + reactions · 3) Cook safely, keep nutrients.

## Quiz
1. What does heat do to food, beyond warming it? *(Changes it chemically — digestible, safer, new flavours.)* 2. What makes bread/cake rise? *(Gas from baking soda or yeast.)* 3. Why cook meat through? *(To kill germs.)*
