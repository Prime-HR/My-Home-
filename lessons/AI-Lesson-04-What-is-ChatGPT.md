# AI For Ghana — Lesson 4: What is ChatGPT and How Do I Use It?
### "A free tutor, writer, and assistant — in your pocket, 24/7"

> **How to use this file:** Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate a slide deck or audio. Each "SLIDE" = one slide. **Say it** = narration / teleprompter.

---

**Lesson:** 4 of 10 (Phase 1 — Foundation) · **Topic:** What ChatGPT is and how to use it
**Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS (layered) · **Duration:** 30–45-sec cut · 45-min class
**By Rauf Husein · AI For Ghana · raufhusein.site**

**By the end you will be able to:**
1. Say what ChatGPT is in plain words.
2. Open it and ask your first question.
3. Use it safely — knowing what NOT to share, and that it can be wrong.

---

## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** Imagine a teacher who answers ANY question, free, any time. 🤖
**Say it:** "Imagine a patient teacher who never sleeps, never charges you, and can explain anything — maths, business, English, life. That's what ChatGPT is. And most Ghanaians have it and don't use it well."

## SLIDE 2 — One line
**On-screen:** ChatGPT = an AI you chat with, like texting a very smart friend. 💬
**Say it:** "ChatGPT is an AI you talk to by typing — like WhatsApp, but the one replying has read almost the whole internet."

## SLIDE 3 — How to get it
**On-screen:** Free at chat.openai.com or the ChatGPT app. 📲
**Say it:** "Open your browser and go to chat.openai.com, or download the ChatGPT app. Create a free account. There's a paid version, but the free one is already powerful enough to change your life."

## SLIDE 4 — How to use it: just ask
**On-screen:** Type what you want — clearly. 🗣️
**Say it:** "Using it is simple — you type a request. 'Explain photosynthesis like I'm 12.' 'Write a polite message asking my landlord for more time.' 'Give me 5 small business ideas for Kumasi.' The clearer you ask, the better it answers."
**🎓 Go deeper:** The instruction you type is called a **prompt** — that's the next phase of this series.

## SLIDE 5 — Real Ghanaian uses
**On-screen:** Learn · Write · Plan · Translate · Brainstorm. ✍️
**Say it:** "A student gets a hard topic explained simply. A trader gets a product advert written. A job seeker improves a CV and cover letter. A pastor or teacher drafts a talk. All in seconds, all free."

## SLIDE 6 — The golden rules of safety
**On-screen:** Never share secrets. Always check important facts. 🔐
**Say it:** "Two rules. One: never type secrets — no MoMo PIN, no passwords, no private ID numbers. Two: it can be confidently wrong, so for anything important — medicine, law, money — verify with a real expert."
**🎓 Go deeper:** When AI states something false as if true, that's a **hallucination**.

## SLIDE 7 — It's a tool, not a master
**On-screen:** Use it to think better — not to stop thinking. 🧠
**Say it:** "ChatGPT should make you sharper, not lazier. Use it to learn and to speed up — but keep your own judgement in charge. You are the boss; it's the assistant."

## SLIDE 8 — Why it matters
**On-screen:** This is the great equaliser for Ghana. 🇬🇭
**Say it:** "A young person in a village with this tool now has the same tutor as a rich kid in a top school. That's the power. Free knowledge, for everyone, finally."

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Ask your first question today. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: ChatGPT is an AI you chat with; it's free; ask clearly; never share secrets; always verify. Challenge: open it today and ask it ONE real question that helps your life. Then teach one person."
**Recap in 3:** 1) It's a free AI you chat with · 2) Ask clearly · 3) Don't share secrets; verify.

## Quick Quiz
1. What is ChatGPT in one sentence? *(An AI you chat with by typing.)*
2. Name 2 things you should NEVER type into it. *(PINs, passwords, private IDs.)*
3. Can ChatGPT be wrong? *(Yes — always verify important things.)*

## Teacher version (45 min)
- 0–5: Ask "If you had a free tutor for any subject, what would you ask?"
- 5–15: Show ChatGPT live on a projector; ask a class question.
- 15–30: Each student writes 3 questions they'd ask; try a few live.
- 30–40: Safety discussion — what not to share; verifying answers.
- 40–45: Homework — ask it one helpful question at home.
