# Pillar 1 · Mathematics — Lesson 16: Profit Margins & Pricing
### "Price too low, you starve. Too high, no sales. The maths of getting it right"

**Subject:** 1.1 Mathematics · **Lesson:** 16 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 (sellers) + SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Calculate profit margin. 2) Set a price from cost and target profit. 3) Avoid the underpricing trap.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Busy all day, selling plenty — but still broke. The price was wrong. 🏷️
**Say it:** "Some sellers are always busy yet never get ahead. The hidden killer is pricing. Price too low and you work for nothing; too high and customers vanish. Today, the maths of the right price."

## SLIDE 2 — Margin vs markup
**On-screen:** Markup = on cost · Margin = on selling price. 📐
**Say it:** "Two words people confuse. Markup is the extra you add ON your cost. Margin is the profit as a share OF your selling price. Knowing the difference protects your profit."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Margin % = (profit ÷ selling price) × 100."

## SLIDE 3 — Start from real cost
**On-screen:** True cost = goods + transport + time + spoilage. 🧾
**Say it:** "Before pricing, know your REAL cost — not just what you paid for goods, but transport, your time, packaging, and spoilage. Underpricing usually comes from forgetting these."

## SLIDE 4 — Price for your target profit
**On-screen:** Cost ₵40, want ₵20 profit → sell ₵60. 🎯
**Say it:** "Decide the profit you need, add it to your true cost, and that's your price. Cost 40, want 20 profit, sell at 60. Simple, deliberate, profitable — not guesswork."

## SLIDE 5 — Test and adjust
**On-screen:** Watch sales; tune the price. 🔄
**Say it:** "Pricing isn't once-and-forever. If everything sells out instantly, you may be too cheap. If nothing moves, maybe too high or you must show more value. Let the numbers guide you."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Right price, real profit. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: know markup vs margin; start from true cost; price for your target profit; then test and adjust. Challenge: re-price one product using its TRUE cost plus the profit you actually need."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Margin vs markup · 2) Use true cost · 3) Add target profit, then test.
