# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 17: Speaking & Pronunciation Clearly
### "It's not about losing your accent — it's about being understood everywhere"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 17 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Speak clearly and be understood. 2) Master commonly confused sounds. 3) Build speaking confidence with pride.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Your accent is beautiful. Clarity is the goal — not imitation. 🗣️
**Say it:** "Let's be clear from the start: your Ghanaian accent is beautiful and you should keep it. The goal isn't to sound foreign — it's to be understood anywhere in the world. Clarity, not imitation. Let's polish how you speak."

## SLIDE 2 — Speak clearly, not fast
**On-screen:** Open your mouth, finish your words. 👄
**Say it:** "The first key to being understood is finishing your words and not rushing. Say the ends of words — 'asked', not 'ask'; 'lists', not 'liss'. Slowing down and opening your mouth makes a huge difference."

## SLIDE 3 — Tricky sounds
**On-screen:** Practise the sounds that get confused. 🔊
**Say it:** "Some English sounds get mixed up. Practise pairs that trip people: 'th' in 'think' and 'this'; the difference between 'ship' and 'sheep'. A few minutes a day on tricky sounds quickly sharpens your speech."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Tongue-twisters are a fun, proven way to train tricky sounds."

## SLIDE 4 — Stress the right part
**On-screen:** PHOto, not phoTO. 📍
**Say it:** "English stresses certain parts of words. We say 'PHO-to', 'im-POR-tant', 'de-VE-lop'. Putting the stress in the right place makes you instantly clearer. Listen and copy good speakers."

## SLIDE 5 — Practise out loud daily
**On-screen:** Read aloud · record · use AI to check. 🔁
**Say it:** "Practice is everything. Read aloud daily, record yourself and listen back, and even ask an AI to help you practise pronunciation, with no judgement. Hearing yourself is how you improve fastest."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Clear, proud, understood. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: keep your accent but aim for clarity; finish your words and don't rush; practise tricky sounds and word stress; and read aloud daily. Challenge: read a paragraph aloud, record it, and listen for words you swallow."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Clarity, not imitation · 2) Finish words; mind tricky sounds · 3) Practise aloud daily.
**CTA:** @AIForGhana · raufhusein.site

## Quiz
1. What's the real goal of good pronunciation? *(To be understood — not to lose your accent.)* 2. Name one way to practise. *(Read aloud, record, tongue-twisters, AI.)* 3. Where is the stress in "important"? *(im-POR-tant.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
Discuss accent vs clarity → finish words → tricky-sound pairs → word stress → tongue-twisters → read-aloud recording.
