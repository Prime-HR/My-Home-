# Pillar 1 · Mathematics — Lesson 12: Averages — Mean, Median & Mode
### "One number can lie. Three numbers tell the truth about your data"

**Objectives:** 1) Find mean, median, and mode. 2) Know when each is fair. 3) Spot misleading "averages".

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** "Average salary is high" — but most people earn little. How? 🤨
**Say it:** "A report says the average income is high, yet most people are struggling. Both can be true — because 'average' has three meanings, and people use the one that suits them. Learn all three and nobody can fool you with numbers."

## SLIDE 2 — Mean: the share-out
**On-screen:** Add all, divide by how many. ➗
**Say it:** "The mean is the everyday average — add all values and divide by the count. Marks of 4, 6, and 8 give a mean of 6. But one huge value can pull the mean up unfairly."

## SLIDE 3 — Median: the middle
**On-screen:** Line them up, take the middle one. 🎯
**Say it:** "The median is the middle value when you line numbers up in order. It ignores extremes, so it often shows the 'typical' person better than the mean — useful for incomes and prices."

## SLIDE 4 — Mode: the most common
**On-screen:** The value that appears most. 🔁
**Say it:** "The mode is simply the most frequent value. A trader wants the mode — which size or item sells most often — to know what to stock."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Mean = sum÷count · Median = middle · Mode = most common."

## SLIDE 5 — Which is fair?
**On-screen:** Extremes? Use median. Most popular? Use mode. ⚖️
**Say it:** "Choose wisely. When a few huge values distort things — like a few very rich people — the median is fairer. For 'what's most popular', use the mode. Anyone quoting only the mean might be hiding something."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Three averages, one clear truth. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: mean is the share-out, median is the middle, mode is the most common; pick the fair one and question single 'averages'. Challenge: collect 7 numbers from your life and find all three."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Mean = sum ÷ count · 2) Median = middle · 3) Mode = most common.
