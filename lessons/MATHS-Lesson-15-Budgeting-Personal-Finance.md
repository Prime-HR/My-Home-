# Pillar 1 · Mathematics — Lesson 15: Budgeting & Personal Finance Math
### "It's not how much you make — it's how much you keep"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.1 Mathematics · **Lesson:** 15 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Build a simple budget. 2) Use the 50/30/20 idea. 3) Make saving a habit with maths.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Two people earn the same. One is broke, one is building. Why? 💰
**Say it:** "Two people earn exactly the same salary. By year's end, one is broke and one has savings and a small business. The difference isn't income — it's budgeting. Let's learn the maths of keeping money."

## SLIDE 2 — A budget = a plan for your money
**On-screen:** Income in, planned out, before you spend. 📋
**Say it:** "A budget is simply deciding where your money goes before it disappears. Write your income at the top, then plan every cedi out — needs, wants, savings. Money without a plan always vanishes."

## SLIDE 3 — The 50/30/20 guide
**On-screen:** 50% needs · 30% wants · 20% savings. 🥧
**Say it:** "A simple guide: aim for about half your money on needs — food, rent, transport; under a third on wants; and at least a fifth on savings. Adjust to your life, but always pay your savings."
**🎓 Go deeper:** These are percentages of your income — Lesson 2's skill in action."

## SLIDE 4 — Pay yourself first
**On-screen:** Save BEFORE you spend, not after. 🏦
**Say it:** "The secret of every saver: take your savings out first, the moment money comes, and live on the rest. If you wait to save 'what's left', there's never anything left."

## SLIDE 5 — Track and adjust
**On-screen:** Use the spreadsheet lesson — watch the leaks. 📊
**Say it:** "Remember the spreadsheet lesson? Track your spending for a month and you'll SEE where money leaks — small daily buys add up. What you measure, you can control."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Plan it, keep it, grow it. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: a budget plans money before you spend; aim roughly 50/30/20; pay savings first; track and adjust. Challenge: write a simple budget for your next income and set your savings amount first."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Plan before you spend · 2) 50/30/20 · 3) Pay yourself first.

## Quiz
1. In 50/30/20, what's the 20% for? *(Savings.)* 2. What does "pay yourself first" mean? *(Save before spending.)* 3. If you earn ₵1000, how much saving at 20%? *(₵200.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
"Where does your money go?" → teach budgeting and 50/30/20 → students build a budget from a sample income → pay-yourself-first discussion → real budget homework.
