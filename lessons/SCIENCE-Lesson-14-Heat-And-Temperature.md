# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 14: Heat & Temperature
### "Why a metal spoon burns but a wooden one doesn't — the science of cooking and weather"

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 14 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Tell heat from temperature. 2) Know the three ways heat travels. 3) Apply it to cooking and staying cool.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Leave a metal and a wooden spoon in hot soup. Only one burns you. Why? 🥄
**Say it:** "Same pot, same heat — but the metal spoon burns your hand and the wooden one doesn't. The answer is the science of heat, and it explains cooking, weather, and even how to keep your room cool."

## SLIDE 2 — Heat vs temperature
**On-screen:** Temperature = how hot · Heat = energy flowing. 🌡️
**Say it:** "Two different things. Temperature is how hot something is — a number. Heat is the energy that flows from hot to cold. Heat always moves from the hotter thing to the cooler one, until they match."

## SLIDE 3 — Three ways heat travels
**On-screen:** Conduction · Convection · Radiation. 🔥
**Say it:** "Heat moves three ways. CONDUCTION — through touching solids, like the metal spoon. CONVECTION — through moving liquids and air, like boiling water rising. RADIATION — straight through space, like the sun's warmth on your skin."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Metals are good **conductors** of heat; wood and air are **insulators**."

## SLIDE 4 — Why the metal spoon burns
**On-screen:** Metal conducts heat fast; wood barely does. 🪵
**Say it:** "Now it's clear: metal conducts heat quickly to your hand, while wood is an insulator and barely passes it. That's why pot handles are wood or plastic, and cooks use cloth to lift hot pots."

## SLIDE 5 — Heat in daily life
**On-screen:** Cooking, drying, weather, keeping cool. ☀️
**Say it:** "Heat science is everywhere: cooking transfers heat into food, the sun dries fish and clothes, hot air rising brings our weather, and light-coloured clothes keep you cooler by reflecting heat."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Control heat, cook and live smarter. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: temperature is how hot, heat is energy flowing from hot to cold; it travels by conduction, convection, and radiation; metals conduct, wood insulates. Challenge: spot one example each of conduction, convection, and radiation in your kitchen."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Heat flows hot→cold · 2) Conduction/convection/radiation · 3) Metals conduct, wood insulates.
