# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 15: Poetry & Figurative Language
### "Why 'her smile was sunshine' moves us more than 'she was happy'"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 15 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Recognise similes, metaphors, and personification. 2) Understand how poetry uses them. 3) Write a short poem.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Our proverbs and songs are full of hidden picture-language. 🎶
**Say it:** "When an elder says 'the rich man's goat gives birth to twins', they mean more than goats. Ghanaian proverbs and songs are full of picture-language. Poetry uses the same magic. Let's unlock how words paint."

## SLIDE 2 — Simile
**On-screen:** Comparing with 'like' or 'as'. 🔍
**Say it:** "A SIMILE compares two things using 'like' or 'as' — 'brave as a lion', 'she ran like the wind'. It paints a quick, vivid picture in the listener's mind."

## SLIDE 3 — Metaphor
**On-screen:** Saying one thing IS another. 🎨
**Say it:** "A METAPHOR goes further — it says something IS another: 'her smile was sunshine', 'time is money', 'the world is a stage'. No 'like' or 'as' — just a bold, beautiful comparison."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Simile uses like/as; metaphor states it directly."

## SLIDE 4 — Personification
**On-screen:** Giving human qualities to non-human things. 🌬️
**Say it:** "PERSONIFICATION gives human traits to things: 'the wind whispered', 'the sun smiled', 'the sea was angry'. It brings the world alive — and our oral tradition does this beautifully."

## SLIDE 5 — Poetry packs feeling
**On-screen:** Few words, big emotion + rhythm. 🥁
**Say it:** "Poetry uses these tools, plus rhythm and sometimes rhyme, to pack huge feeling into few words — like our songs and drumming. A great poem can say in four lines what a page cannot."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Make words paint and sing. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: similes compare with like/as, metaphors say one thing is another, personification gives things human traits, and poetry packs feeling into few words. Challenge: write one simile and one metaphor about your hometown."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Simile (like/as) · 2) Metaphor (is) · 3) Personification (human traits).

## Quiz
1. "Brave as a lion" is a…? *(Simile.)* 2. "Time is money" is a…? *(Metaphor.)* 3. "The wind whispered" is…? *(Personification.)*

Share local proverbs/songs → similes → metaphors → personification → write a short poem with each device.
