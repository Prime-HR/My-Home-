# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 7: Verb Tenses — Getting Time Right
### "Past, present, future — say exactly when, and never confuse your listener"

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 7 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Use past, present, and future correctly. 2) Keep tense consistent. 3) Fix common tense errors.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** "Yesterday I go to Kumasi" — small word, big mistake. ⏳
**Say it:** "One wrong verb can make you sound less educated than you are. 'Yesterday I go' should be 'Yesterday I went.' Tenses tell time, and getting them right makes your English clear and confident. Let's master time."

## SLIDE 2 — The three times
**On-screen:** Past · Present · Future. 🕐
**Say it:** "Verbs change with time. PAST — it already happened (I walked). PRESENT — it happens now or usually (I walk). FUTURE — it will happen (I will walk). Choose the time, choose the verb."

## SLIDE 3 — Past tense
**On-screen:** Usually add -ed… but watch the tricky ones. 📅
**Say it:** "Most past verbs add -ed: walk → walked, talk → talked. But many common ones change completely: go → went, eat → ate, buy → bought. These you simply must learn — and they trip up many people."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Verbs that change form (go→went) are called **irregular verbs**."

## SLIDE 4 — Present & future
**On-screen:** I eat (now) · I will eat (later). ➡️
**Say it:** "Present is for now and habits: 'I eat banku on Fridays.' Future uses 'will' or 'going to': 'I will eat,' 'I am going to eat.' Simple signposts for what's coming."

## SLIDE 5 — Stay consistent
**On-screen:** Don't jump times in one story. 🔄
**Say it:** "Pick a time and stay in it. Don't write 'I went to the shop and I buy bread' — keep it past: 'I went to the shop and bought bread.' Consistent tense keeps your reader steady."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Right tense, clear meaning. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: past, present, and future change the verb; most past verbs add -ed but irregulars change; stay consistent in one piece. Challenge: write one sentence in each tense about going to the market."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Past/present/future · 2) Learn irregular verbs · 3) Stay consistent.

## Quiz
1. Past tense of "go"? *(Went.)* 2. Make future: "I eat rice." *(I will eat rice.)* 3. Fix: "Yesterday she buy bread." *(Yesterday she bought bread.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
Tell a story, spot the tense → teach three tenses → irregular-verb drill → fix mixed-tense sentences → one-sentence-each homework.
