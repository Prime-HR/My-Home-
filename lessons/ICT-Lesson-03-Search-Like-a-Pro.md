## SLIDE 2 — The mindset shift
**On-screen:** Searching is a **skill** — and skills can be learned. 🧠

**Say it:** "Two people, same phone, same Google. One finds the answer in 10 seconds; the other gives up. The difference is technique. A good searcher can teach themselves almost anything. Let me give you the techniques the smart ones use."

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** The words you type are called **keywords** — choosing them well is the whole game.

---

## SLIDE 3 — Trick 1: Use keywords, not long stories
**On-screen:** ❌ "Please where can I find the BECE timetable for this year?" → ✅ "BECE timetable 2026"

**Say it:** "First rule: don't type like you're begging a person. Type only the important words. Instead of a long sentence, use the 3 or 4 words that matter. 'BECE timetable 2026.' 'Cement price Ghana.' 'NHIS renewal online.' Short, sharp keywords beat long sentences every time."

**🇬🇭 Try it:** "passport application Ghana cost" beats "how much money do I need to apply for a passport in Ghana please".

---

## SLIDE 4 — Trick 2: Quotes for exact words
**On-screen:** Put "exact words" in quotes to find them EXACTLY. 🎯

**Say it:** "Powerful one. Put quotation marks around words and Google finds that exact phrase, in that exact order. Search \"WASSCE 2024 timetable\" with quotes and you cut out all the noise. Use it when the normal search gives you everything except what you wanted."

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** Quotes are a **search operator** — a special instruction that controls how the search works.

---

## SLIDE 5 — Trick 3: Search inside one site + find files
**On-screen:** `site:` searches one website. `filetype:pdf` finds documents. 📄

**Say it:** "Two professional tricks. Type 'site:' then a website to search only inside it — for example 'scholarship site:ug.edu.gh' searches only the University of Ghana's site. And add 'filetype:pdf' to find documents — 'BECE past questions filetype:pdf' will dig out the actual PDFs. This is how serious students find past questions and notes."

**🇬🇭 Gold:** `past questions integrated science filetype:pdf` — free exam practice, instantly.

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** Other operators: a **minus sign** removes a word (`jaguar -car` = the animal, not the car), and the **Tools → time filter** shows only recent results.

---

## SLIDE 6 — Trick 4: The right tool for the question
**On-screen:** Don't only use Google search. 🔧

**Say it:** "Match the tool to the question. Need to learn a skill by watching? YouTube. Need a place or directions? Google Maps. Got a photo and want to know if it's real or where it's from? Use reverse image search — upload the picture and Google tells you where else it appears. Need something explained simply or summarised? Ask an AI like ChatGPT."

- ▶️ **YouTube** — learn any skill by watching
- 🗺️ **Maps** — places, directions, opening hours
- 🖼️ **Reverse image search** — check if a photo is fake or old
- 🤖 **AI (ChatGPT)** — explain, summarise, draft

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** Checking a picture with **reverse image search** is a key way to spot fake news.

---

## SLIDE 7 — The most important skill: is it TRUE?
**On-screen:** Finding information is easy. Knowing what to TRUST is the real skill. ⚠️

**Say it:** "Here's what separates a wise searcher from a foolish one. Not everything online is true — especially WhatsApp forwards and shocking headlines. Before you believe or share anything: check WHO is saying it, look for the same news on 2 or 3 trusted sites, and be extra careful when something makes you very angry or very excited — that's often how fake news spreads. Verify before you share."

**🇬🇭 Golden rule:** "If it's not on 2–3 serious sources, don't share it."

**🎓 Go deeper (students):** Judging if a source is reliable is called checking **source credibility** — a core digital-literacy skill.

## Teacher version (45 minutes)
- **0–5 min — Starter:** Ask one student to find the BECE/WASSCE timetable on the projector. Watch *how* they search.
- **5–18 min — Instruction:** Teach keywords, quotes, site:, filetype:pdf (Slides 3–5) with live demos.
- **18–30 min — Search race:** Give 5 real questions; first to find each with a trusted source wins. (e.g., "find a past question PDF for your subject".)
- **30–40 min — Truth test:** Show one true and one fake story; class verifies which is real and explains how.
- **40–45 min — Wrap:** Each student writes their 3 favourite tricks to keep.
