## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** Buy too little cement, you stop work. Buy too much, you waste money. Maths fixes both. 🧱
**Say it:** "Don't scroll. The tailor who measures wrong loses cloth. The builder who guesses wrong loses money. The cook who can't estimate burns food and cash. Measurement maths quietly decides who wastes and who wins."

## SLIDE 2 — One line
**On-screen:** Measurement = giving size a number. 📏
**Say it:** "Measurement just means putting a number on size — how long, how heavy, how much. Get the right number and everything else works."

## SLIDE 3 — The four you'll use most
**On-screen:** Length · Weight · Volume · Area. 📐
**Say it:** "Four kinds matter daily. LENGTH — metres and centimetres for cloth and rooms. WEIGHT — kilograms for food and goods. VOLUME — litres for fuel, water, drinks. AREA — for land, walls, and floors."

## SLIDE 4 — Estimation: your anti-cheat tool
**On-screen:** A rough answer in your head beats being fooled. 🧠
**Say it:** "You don't always need an exact number — a good estimate protects you. If a trader says a small bag is '5 kilos', and you know 5 kilos feels heavier, you ask questions. Estimating keeps you sharp at the market."
**🎓 Go deeper (students):** Estimation = a sensible rough answer; great for checking if an exact answer makes sense.

## SLIDE 5 — Area: length × width
**On-screen:** A room 4m by 3m = 12 square metres. ▭
**Say it:** "Area is length times width. A room 4 metres by 3 metres is 12 square metres. Now you know how many tiles, how much paint, how much cloth — no more guessing and wasting."

## SLIDE 6 — Real Ghana jobs
**On-screen:** Cloth, land plots, cement bags, paint tins. 🏗️
**Say it:** "This is daily money. A seamstress works out cloth from body measurements. A builder estimates blocks and cement from wall area. A landowner checks plot size before buying. Wrong maths here costs real cedis."

## SLIDE 7 — Convert without fear
**On-screen:** 1 metre = 100 cm · 1 kg = 1000 g · 1 litre = 1000 ml. 🔄
**Say it:** "Conversions are simple once you know the key ones: 100 centimetres in a metre, 1000 grams in a kilo, 1000 millilitres in a litre. Learn these few and you can switch units anytime."

## SLIDE 8 — Why it matters
**On-screen:** Measure right, spend right. 💰
**Say it:** "Every building, every dress, every meal, every plot of land runs on measurement. The person who measures and estimates well saves money on every single job. It's quiet power."

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Measure twice, pay once. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: measure length, weight, volume, area; estimate to avoid being cheated; area is length times width; know your conversions. Challenge: measure one real thing today — your room's area — and work out how much it would cost to tile or paint it."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Length, weight, volume, area · 2) Estimate to stay sharp · 3) Area = length × width.

## Quick Quiz
1. What is the area of a 5m × 4m room? *(20 square metres.)*
2. How many grams in 2 kilograms? *(2000 g.)*
3. Why is estimation useful at the market? *(To sense-check sizes/prices and avoid being cheated.)*

## Teacher version (45 min)
- 0–5: "Who has been short-changed on quantity?" Discuss.
- 5–18: Teach the four measures and key conversions.
- 18–30: Measure the classroom; calculate its area together.
- 30–40: Estimation game — guess then measure objects.
- 40–45: Homework — measure a room and estimate a tiling cost.
