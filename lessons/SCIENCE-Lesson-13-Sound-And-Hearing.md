# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 13: Sound & Hearing
### "Every drum, song, and voice is just air shaking — here's the science"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 13 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Explain how sound is made and travels. 2) Understand how we hear. 3) Protect your hearing.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** A drum, a song, a voice — all are shaking air. 🥁
**Say it:** "Our music moves the world, but what IS sound? When you hear highlife, a church choir, or your mother calling — your ears are catching shaking air. Let's understand the science of every sound you love."

## SLIDE 2 — Sound = vibration
**On-screen:** Something vibrates → the air vibrates → you hear. 〰️
**Say it:** "Every sound starts with something vibrating — a drum skin, vocal cords, a speaker. That shaking pushes the air in waves, and when those waves reach your ear, you hear sound."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Sound travels as **waves**; faster vibrations make higher pitch."

## SLIDE 3 — Sound needs a material
**On-screen:** It travels through air, water, and solids — not empty space. 🌊
**Say it:** "Sound must travel through something — air, water, or solid. That's why you can hear footsteps through the ground. In outer space, where there's no air, there's total silence."

## SLIDE 4 — How we hear
**On-screen:** Ear catches waves → eardrum vibrates → brain hears. 👂
**Say it:** "Your outer ear funnels sound waves to the eardrum, which vibrates and passes the signal deeper in, then to the brain, which understands it as a voice or a song. A delicate, brilliant system."

## SLIDE 5 — Loudness and danger
**On-screen:** Too loud, too long = permanent damage. 🔊
**Say it:** "Loud sound carries strong waves that can damage the ear for life. Very loud music in earphones, blasting speakers, and noisy machines slowly harm hearing — and once gone, it rarely comes back."

## SLIDE 6 — Protect your hearing
**On-screen:** Lower the volume · take breaks · protect at loud events. 🎧
**Say it:** "Guard your ears: keep earphone volume moderate, rest from loud sound, and use protection around very loud machines or events. Your future self will thank you."

## SLIDE 7 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Hear the science in every beat. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: sound is vibration carried by waves through a material; the ear turns waves into hearing; loud sound can damage it forever; protect your ears. Challenge: feel a speaker or your throat vibrate as it makes sound."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Sound = vibration/waves · 2) Needs a material to travel · 3) Protect against loud sound.

## Quiz
1. What makes sound? *(Vibration.)* 2. Can sound travel in empty space? *(No — it needs a material.)* 3. What does very loud sound do to ears? *(Can permanently damage hearing.)*
