# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 6: Grammar 2 — Building Correct Sentences
### "A clear sentence is the difference between being understood and being ignored"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 6 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Build a complete sentence. 2) Match subject and verb. 3) Avoid run-ons and fragments.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** "Going market now." vs "I am going to the market now." Which gets respect? ✍️
**Say it:** "We all understand broken English among friends. But in a job, an exam, or business, a complete, correct sentence earns respect and opens doors. Let's learn to build sentences that work everywhere."

## SLIDE 2 — What makes a sentence
**On-screen:** A subject + a verb + a complete thought. 🧱
**Say it:** "A real sentence needs a SUBJECT (who or what) and a VERB (the action), and it must express a complete thought. 'The teacher teaches.' Subject, verb, complete. Done right, it stands on its own."

## SLIDE 3 — Subject–verb agreement
**On-screen:** "He goes" not "He go". 🤝
**Say it:** "The subject and verb must agree. One person: 'He goes, she eats, it works.' Many: 'They go, we eat.' This small rule is the most common slip in Ghanaian English — fix it and your English jumps a level."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Singular subjects usually take a verb ending in -s (he runs); plural ones don't (they run)."

## SLIDE 4 — Avoid fragments
**On-screen:** "Because I was late." — incomplete! ⚠️
**Say it:** "A fragment is a piece pretending to be a sentence — 'Because I was late.' It leaves the reader waiting. Finish the thought: 'I missed the bus because I was late.'"

## SLIDE 5 — Avoid run-ons
**On-screen:** Don't cram everything into one breathless line. 🛑
**Say it:** "The opposite error is the run-on — joining many ideas without proper breaks: 'I went to school I was tired I slept.' Break it up: 'I went to school. I was tired, so I slept.' Clear beats crammed."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Complete, agreeing, clear. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: a sentence needs a subject, a verb, and a complete thought; subject and verb must agree; avoid fragments and run-ons. Challenge: fix this — 'Going town buy things.' Write the full, correct sentence."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Subject + verb + complete thought · 2) They must agree · 3) No fragments or run-ons.
