**Objectives:** 1) Run a simple investigation. 2) Understand innovation. 3) See yourself as a scientist for Ghana.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Every great scientist was once a curious child — just like you. 🔬
**Say it:** "We've journeyed from the cell to space, from soil to robots. Now the most important lesson: YOU can be a scientist. Not someday — now. Every breakthrough began with a curious person asking 'why' and trying. Let's make that you."

## SLIDE 2 — The scientist's method
**On-screen:** Ask → guess → test → observe → conclude. 🔁
**Say it:** "Remember Lesson 1? A scientist asks a question, makes a smart guess, tests it fairly, observes carefully, and draws a conclusion. You can apply this to anything — a better stove, a cheaper water filter, a healthier crop."

## SLIDE 3 — Do your own experiment
**On-screen:** Pick a question. Test it. Record it. 📝
**Say it:** "Start small. 'Does adding ash help plants grow?' Plant two pots, one with ash, one without, treat them the same, and watch. Record what happens. That's real science, with materials from your own home."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Keep everything the same except the one thing you're testing — that's a **fair test**."

## SLIDE 4 — From science to innovation
**On-screen:** Solving a real problem = innovation. 💡
**Say it:** "Innovation is using science to solve a real problem. The Ghanaians turning plastic into blocks, building solar dryers, and creating health apps are innovators. Look around — every problem is an invitation to invent."

## SLIDE 5 — Science needs you, Ghana needs you
**On-screen:** Our challenges await your curiosity. 🇬🇭
**Say it:** "Ghana's future doctors, engineers, farmers, and inventors are sitting in classrooms today. Your curiosity is the spark. With effort and this foundation, you can solve problems that defeated others."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Be curious. Test. Build. Repeat. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: the scientific method is your tool; you can run real experiments at home; innovation means solving real problems; and Ghana needs your curiosity. Challenge: choose one question you've always wondered, design a fair test, and try it this week."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Use the method anywhere · 2) Run a fair test · 3) Innovate to solve real problems.
