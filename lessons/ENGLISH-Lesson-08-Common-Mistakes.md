## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** These tiny mistakes quietly cost people jobs and marks. 🔍
**Say it:** "Even fluent speakers make a handful of common slips. The good news: fix just a few and you instantly sound sharper in interviews, exams, and business. Let's clean up the usual culprits — kindly, no shame."

## SLIDE 2 — "I am coming" vs the moment
**On-screen:** Match the words to the real meaning. 🗣️
**Say it:** "We often say 'I'm coming' while walking away, or 'take it' for 'bring it'. In wider English these confuse people. Aim for what the words actually mean — small shifts that travel better beyond home."

## SLIDE 3 — Plurals & agreement
**On-screen:** "Two boys" not "two boy"; "they are" not "they is". 👥
**Say it:** "Remember to mark plurals: 'three books', not 'three book'. And match them: 'they are', 'the children play'. This is one of the most common and most fixable slips."

## SLIDE 4 — Confusing word pairs
**On-screen:** Their/there/they're · your/you're · its/it's. 🔁
**Say it:** "Watch these famous pairs. THEIR (belonging), THERE (place), THEY'RE (they are). YOUR (belonging), YOU'RE (you are). Knowing them makes your writing look careful and professional."
**🎓 Go deeper:** When unsure, expand it: 'you're' = 'you are' — does it still make sense?"

## SLIDE 5 — Prepositions & extra words
**On-screen:** "Discuss it" not "discuss about it". 🎯
**Say it:** "Trim extra words: we 'discuss something', not 'discuss about it'; we 'reach a place', not 'reach to a place'. Cleaner English is correct English. Less is often more."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Fix the small things, win big. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: match words to meaning, mark plurals and agreement, master their/there/they're and your/you're, and trim extra words. Challenge: write one sentence using 'they're', 'their', and 'there' correctly."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Mark plurals & agreement · 2) Master tricky word pairs · 3) Trim extra words.

## Quiz
1. Their, there, or they're: "___ going home." *(They're.)* 2. Correct: "Discuss about the plan." *(Discuss the plan.)* 3. Fix: "I have two pen." *(I have two pens.)*
