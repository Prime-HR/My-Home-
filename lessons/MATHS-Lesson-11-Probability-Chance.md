# Pillar 1 · Mathematics — Lesson 11: Probability & Chance
### "The honest maths of lotto, risk, and 'what are the odds?'"

**Subject:** 1.1 Mathematics · **Lesson:** 11 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Understand probability from 0 to 1. 2) Judge risk honestly. 3) See why "the house always wins".

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Why does the lotto seller always win, and rarely the player? 🎲
**Say it:** "People spend their last cedi 'staking' on lotto, sure that today is their day. The maths tells a harder truth. Understanding probability protects your money and helps you judge any risk wisely."

## SLIDE 2 — Probability from 0 to 1
**On-screen:** 0 = never · 1 = certain · 1/2 = 50/50. 📊
**Say it:** "Probability measures how likely something is, from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain). Flipping a coin for heads is 1/2 — a fifty-fifty chance. Everything risky sits somewhere on this scale."

## SLIDE 3 — Counting the chances
**On-screen:** Favourable outcomes ÷ all outcomes. 🔢
**Say it:** "To find probability, divide the outcomes you want by all possible outcomes. One red ball among five balls gives a 1 in 5 chance — that's 0.2, or 20%."
**🎓 Go deeper:** P(event) = favourable outcomes ÷ total outcomes."

## SLIDE 4 — Why the house wins
**On-screen:** Games are designed so YOU lose slowly. 🏦
**Say it:** "Lotto and betting are built so that, over many plays, the seller keeps more than they pay out. You might win once, but the maths quietly takes more than it gives. The 'house edge' is real."

## SLIDE 5 — Risk in real life
**On-screen:** Weather, health, business — weigh the odds. 🌦️
**Say it:** "Probability isn't only games. 'There's a high chance of rain' — carry an umbrella. 'This business is risky' — count the odds before you spend. Thinking in probability makes you a wiser decision-maker."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Know the odds, keep your money. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: probability runs 0 to 1; it's favourable over total outcomes; betting is designed for you to lose over time; weigh real risks with odds. Challenge: work out the probability of one simple event today — like picking a particular item from a bag."
**Recap in 3:** 1) 0 = never, 1 = certain · 2) Favourable ÷ total · 3) The house is built to win.

## Quiz
