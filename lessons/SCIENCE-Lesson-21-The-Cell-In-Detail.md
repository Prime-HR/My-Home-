# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 21: The Cell in Detail
### "The smallest living thing — yet it does everything you do"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 21 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 + curious adults · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Name the main parts of a cell. 2) Tell a plant cell from an animal cell. 3) Link cells to health.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** You are built from 30 trillion tiny living machines. 🔬
**Say it:** "Every part of you — skin, blood, brain — is built from cells, each a tiny living machine doing a job. Understand the cell and you understand life itself, and your own health."

## SLIDE 2 — The cell is alive
**On-screen:** It feeds, grows, works, and divides. 🧫
**Say it:** "A single cell does what you do in miniature — it takes in food, releases energy, removes waste, and makes copies of itself. It's the smallest unit of life."

## SLIDE 3 — Main parts
**On-screen:** Membrane · cytoplasm · nucleus. 🧬
**Say it:** "Three key parts: the MEMBRANE is the skin controlling what enters; the CYTOPLASM is the jelly where work happens; the NUCLEUS is the control centre holding the DNA instructions."
**🎓 Go deeper:** The **nucleus** is the 'brain'; the **mitochondria** are the 'power stations' making energy."

## SLIDE 4 — Plant vs animal cells
**On-screen:** Plant cells have a wall + chloroplasts. 🌿
**Say it:** "Plant cells have extras: a tough cell wall for support and green chloroplasts for photosynthesis. Animal cells have neither. That's why plants stand firm and make their own food."

## SLIDE 5 — Cells team up
**On-screen:** Cells → tissues → organs → you. 🧱
**Say it:** "Cells of one kind group into tissues, tissues form organs like the heart, and organs build the whole body. Damage at the cell level shows up as disease in the whole person."

## SLIDE 6 — Why it matters
**On-screen:** Cancer, healing, medicine all start at the cell. 🩺
**Say it:** "Healing a wound, fighting cancer, how medicines work — all happen at the cell level. The scientists who understand cells are the ones who cure diseases."

## SLIDE 7 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Life begins in the cell. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: cells are living units; membrane, cytoplasm, nucleus are key parts; plant cells add a wall and chloroplasts; cells build tissues and organs. Challenge: draw a simple cell and label its three main parts."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Cell = unit of life · 2) Membrane/cytoplasm/nucleus · 3) Plant cells have wall + chloroplasts.
**CTA:** @AIForGhana · raufhusein.site

## Quiz
1. What part controls the cell? *(Nucleus.)* 2. Two things only plant cells have? *(Cell wall, chloroplasts.)* 3. Cells group to form what? *(Tissues.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
"What are you made of?" → teach cell parts → plant vs animal → cells→tissues→organs → draw and label a cell.
