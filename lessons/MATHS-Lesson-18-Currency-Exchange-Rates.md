# Pillar 1 · Mathematics — Lesson 18: Currency & Exchange Rates
### "Why the cedi and the dollar dance — and how to do the maths"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.1 Mathematics · **Lesson:** 18 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Understand what an exchange rate is. 2) Convert between cedis and dollars. 3) See how rates affect prices and remittances.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Why do prices jump when "the dollar goes up"? 💵🇬🇭
**Say it:** "You hear it constantly: 'the dollar has gone up, so prices have gone up.' But what does that really mean, and how do you do the maths? Understand exchange rates and you understand half of Ghana's economy news."

## SLIDE 2 — What is an exchange rate?
**On-screen:** How much of one currency = one of another. 🔄
**Say it:** "An exchange rate tells you how many cedis it takes to buy one dollar. If the rate is 15, then one dollar costs 15 cedis. It's just a conversion number."

## SLIDE 3 — Converting money
**On-screen:** Cedis → dollars: divide. Dollars → cedis: multiply. ➗✖️
**Say it:** "To change 150 cedis into dollars at a rate of 15, divide: that's 10 dollars. To change 20 dollars into cedis, multiply by 15: 300 cedis. Multiply one way, divide the other."
**🎓 Go deeper:** This is just ratios and proportion from Lesson 5, applied to money."

## SLIDE 4 — Why rates change
**On-screen:** Demand, exports, imports, confidence. 📉📈
**Say it:** "Rates move with supply and demand. When Ghana imports a lot and exports less, we need more dollars, so the dollar costs more cedis — the cedi 'weakens'. Strong exports do the opposite."

## SLIDE 5 — Why it touches your life
**On-screen:** Imported goods, fuel, remittances from abroad. 🌍
**Say it:** "Most goods we buy are imported, priced in dollars — so when the cedi weakens, prices rise. And family sending money home from abroad? The rate decides how many cedis arrive. This maths is personal."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Read the rate, understand the price. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: an exchange rate converts currencies; divide to get dollars, multiply to get cedis; rates move with trade and confidence; they shape prices and remittances. Challenge: convert today's price of one dollar into and out of cedis."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Rate = conversion number · 2) Divide ↔ multiply · 3) It moves prices & remittances.

## Quiz
1. At a rate of 12, how many cedis is $5? *(₵60.)* 2. At a rate of 12, how many dollars is ₵120? *($10.)* 3. When the cedi weakens, what happens to import prices? *(They rise.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
Look up today's rate → teach conversion both ways → practice problems → discuss why prices rise with the dollar → real conversion homework.
