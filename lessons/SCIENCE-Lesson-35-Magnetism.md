# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 35: Magnetism & Its Uses
### "An invisible force that guides ships, spins motors, and makes electricity"

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 35 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Understand magnets and poles. 2) Link magnetism to electricity. 3) Recognise magnetism in daily devices.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** The same invisible force in a toy magnet powers the whole electric world. 🧲
**Say it:** "A magnet pulling a pin seems like a toy. But that same invisible force guides ships, spins every electric motor, and generates the electricity in your home. Magnetism is one of nature's quiet giants."

## SLIDE 2 — Magnets and poles
**On-screen:** Two poles: North and South. ↔️
**Say it:** "Every magnet has two ends — a North pole and a South pole. Opposite poles attract, like poles push apart. This simple rule is the seed of huge technology."

## SLIDE 3 — Magnetic materials
**On-screen:** Iron, steel, nickel respond; most don't. 🔩
**Say it:** "Only some materials are magnetic — mainly iron, steel, and nickel. Wood, plastic, and gold are not. That's why a magnet picks up a nail but not a coin made of other metal."

## SLIDE 4 — The compass
**On-screen:** Earth itself is a giant magnet. 🧭
**Say it:** "The Earth behaves like a giant magnet, which is why a compass needle always points North. For centuries this guided travellers and sailors — direction from an invisible force."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Electricity and magnetism are linked — together called **electromagnetism**."

## SLIDE 5 — Magnetism + electricity = power
**On-screen:** Motors spin; generators make electricity. ⚙️⚡
**Say it:** "The big secret: moving magnets near wires create electricity, and electricity through wires creates magnetism. This partnership runs electric motors in fans and cars, and the generators at Akosombo that power Ghana."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Tiny force, huge power. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: magnets have North and South poles; opposites attract; only some metals are magnetic; Earth is a magnet guiding compasses; and magnetism plus electricity runs motors and generators. Challenge: test what a magnet picks up at home and sort magnetic from non-magnetic."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Two poles; opposites attract · 2) Iron/steel/nickel are magnetic · 3) Magnetism + electricity = motors & power.

## Quiz
1. What are the two ends of a magnet called? *(North and South poles.)* 2. Name a magnetic metal. *(Iron, steel, or nickel.)* 3. What do generators use to make electricity? *(Magnetism — moving magnets and wires.)*

Play with magnets → poles & attraction → test magnetic materials → compass + Earth → motors/generators link.
