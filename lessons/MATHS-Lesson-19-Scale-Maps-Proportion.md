# Pillar 1 · Mathematics — Lesson 19: Scale, Maps & Proportion
### "How a whole city fits on one page — and a recipe feeds 200"

**Subject:** 1.1 Mathematics · **Lesson:** 19 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Understand scale. 2) Read a map distance. 3) Scale recipes and plans up or down.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** How does the whole of Accra fit on one small map? 🗺️
**Say it:** "A map shrinks a whole region onto one page, yet you can still measure real distances from it. The same maths lets a cook feed 200 from a recipe for 4. It's called scale — and it's everywhere."

## SLIDE 2 — What is scale?
**On-screen:** A fixed shrink (or grow) factor. 📐
**Say it:** "Scale means everything is shrunk or grown by the same factor. A map scale of 1 to 100,000 means 1 centimetre on paper equals 100,000 centimetres — one kilometre — on the ground."

## SLIDE 3 — Reading map distance
**On-screen:** Measure on map × scale = real distance. 📏
**Say it:** "Measure the distance on the map, then multiply by the scale to get the real distance. This is how planners, drivers, and builders read maps and plans accurately."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Scale is proportion (Lesson 5) — keeping the same ratio everywhere."

## SLIDE 4 — Scaling recipes & plans
**On-screen:** Recipe for 4 → for 200: multiply every part. 🍲
**Say it:** "Cooking for a wedding? Take the recipe for 4 and multiply every ingredient by the same number to reach 200. Keep the ratio and the taste stays right. Same idea scales a building plan or a business."

## SLIDE 5 — Why it matters
**On-screen:** Maps, building, catering, design — all scale. 🏗️
**Say it:** "Architects draw scaled plans; caterers scale recipes; tailors scale patterns; designers scale images. Master scale and you can plan anything big from something small."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Shrink it, grow it, keep it true. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: scale shrinks or grows by a fixed factor; multiply map distance by scale for the real one; scale recipes and plans by the same factor. Challenge: scale a recipe from its normal size up to feed 20 people."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Scale = fixed factor · 2) Map × scale = real · 3) Scale recipes/plans proportionally.

## Quiz
1. On a 1:100,000 map, 2 cm = how many km? *(2 km.)* 2. A recipe for 4 scaled to 12 — multiply by? *(3.)* 3. Scale keeps what the same? *(The ratio/proportion.)*

Look at a real map and scale → measure and convert a distance → scale a simple recipe → discuss plans and patterns → scale-a-recipe homework.
