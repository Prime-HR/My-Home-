# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 29: States of Matter & Particle Theory
### "Everything is made of tiny moving particles — and that explains everything"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 29 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Describe particles in solids, liquids, gases. 2) Explain changes of state by particles. 3) Apply it to everyday life.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Why does perfume fill a whole room? Tiny particles on the move. 💨
**Say it:** "Open perfume in one corner and soon the whole room smells it. Drop sugar in water and it vanishes. The secret to all of this is that everything is made of tiny moving particles. Master this idea and chemistry makes sense."

## SLIDE 2 — Everything is particles
**On-screen:** Tiny particles, always moving. ⚛️
**Say it:** "Every substance is made of tiny particles too small to see, and they are always moving. How closely they're packed and how fast they move decides whether something is solid, liquid, or gas."

## SLIDE 3 — Solids, liquids, gases by particles
**On-screen:** Solid (packed) · Liquid (loose) · Gas (free). 🧊
**Say it:** "In a SOLID, particles are packed tight and only vibrate — so it holds shape. In a LIQUID, they're looser and slide — so it flows. In a GAS, they're far apart and zoom freely — so it spreads everywhere."
**🎓 Go deeper:** More heat = faster particles = looser arrangement (this is **particle theory**)."

## SLIDE 4 — Changes of state explained
**On-screen:** Heat loosens; cooling tightens. 🔥❄️
**Say it:** "Now changes of state make sense. Heat gives particles energy to break free — ice melts, water boils. Cooling slows them so they settle — steam condenses, water freezes. Same particles, just more or less energy."

## SLIDE 5 — Why perfume and cooking smells spread
**On-screen:** Gas particles mix into the air. 🌸
**Say it:** "Smells spread because gas particles from the perfume or the cooking pot fly out and mix into the moving air, reaching your nose across the room. That spreading is called diffusion."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Tiny particles, big explanations. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: everything is moving particles; solids pack tight, liquids loosen, gases fly free; heat and cooling change states; smells spread by diffusion. Challenge: explain melting ice using particles in your own words."
**Recap in 3:** 1) All matter = moving particles · 2) Packing decides solid/liquid/gas · 3) Heat loosens, cooling tightens.

## Quiz
1. In which state are particles packed tightest? *(Solid.)* 2. What happens to particles when you heat them? *(They move faster and spread.)* 3. Why does perfume fill a room? *(Diffusion of gas particles.)*
