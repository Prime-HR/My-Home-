# AI For Ghana — Lesson 5: Can AI Speak Twi?
### "Why AI struggles with our languages — and how Ghana can fix it"

> **How to use this file:** Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate a slide deck or audio. Each "SLIDE" = one slide. **Say it** = narration / teleprompter.

---

**Lesson:** 5 of 10 (Phase 1 — Foundation) · **Topic:** AI and Ghanaian languages (NLP)
**Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS (layered) · **Duration:** 30–45-sec cut · 45-min class
**By Rauf Husein · AI For Ghana · raufhusein.site**

**By the end you will be able to:**
1. Understand how AI handles human language (NLP), in simple terms.
2. Explain why AI is weaker in Twi, Ewe, Ga, Dagbani than in English.
3. See the opportunity for Ghanaians to build local-language AI.

---

## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** Ask AI something in Twi. It tries… but stumbles. Why? 🗣️
**Say it:** "Type a message to AI in Twi and watch. Sometimes it gets it; often it stumbles. English? Perfect. Twi? Shaky. Why does a 'super-intelligent' AI struggle with our mother tongue? The answer matters for Ghana's future."

## SLIDE 2 — One line
**On-screen:** AI learns a language by reading mountains of it. 📚
**Say it:** "Remember Lesson 2 — AI learns from examples. For language, the 'examples' are written text. The more text in a language, the better AI speaks it."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Teaching machines to handle language is called **NLP — Natural Language Processing**.

## SLIDE 3 — The English advantage
**On-screen:** Billions of English pages online. AI feasted. 🍽️
**Say it:** "There are billions of English web pages, books, and articles. AI studied all of it, so its English is excellent. It had a giant feast."

## SLIDE 4 — The Twi problem
**On-screen:** Far less Twi, Ewe, Ga, Dagbani is written online. 📉
**Say it:** "Our languages are rich and spoken by millions — but much of that lives in speech, not in writing online. So AI had only crumbs to study. Less data, weaker results. It's not that our languages are 'lesser' — they're just under-represented on the internet."

## SLIDE 5 — Code-switching trick
**On-screen:** Mix Twi + English and many tools cope better. 🔀
**Say it:** "A practical tip: today, mixing Twi and English — the way we naturally speak — often works better with AI than pure deep Twi. Try it. It's improving fast."

## SLIDE 6 — Why this is a BIG deal
**On-screen:** Language = access. No Twi AI = millions left out. ⚖️
**Say it:** "If AI only works well in English, then the farmer or trader who thinks in Twi is locked out of the AI revolution. Language is the door. We must make sure the door opens for every Ghanaian."

## SLIDE 7 — Ghana's golden opportunity
**On-screen:** Who will build the Twi AI? Maybe YOU. 🇬🇭🚀
**Say it:** "Here's the exciting part. This gap is an opportunity. Ghanaians who record, write, and digitise our languages, and who help train AI on them, will build something the world needs — and preserve our heritage at the same time."

## SLIDE 8 — How you can help today
**On-screen:** Write, record, and use our languages online. ✍️
**Say it:** "Even small acts help: write in Twi online, record proverbs and folktales, support local language projects. Every bit of good data teaches the AI of tomorrow to speak like us."

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** AI can learn Twi — if we feed it Twi. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: AI learns language from text; our languages have less text online; that's a gap and an opportunity. Challenge: write one proverb in your language online today, and explain its meaning."
**Recap in 3:** 1) AI learns language from data · 2) Our languages have less online · 3) We can change that.

## Quick Quiz
1. What is NLP? *(Natural Language Processing — AI handling human language.)*
2. Why is AI weaker in Twi than English? *(Far less written Twi data online.)*
3. Name one way to help. *(Write/record our languages; support local-language AI.)*

## Teacher version (45 min)
- 0–5: Try a Twi prompt live; observe results.
- 5–18: Explain NLP and the data gap simply.
- 18–30: Activity — students translate 5 proverbs to English and discuss nuances AI might miss.
- 30–40: Debate — should AI learn our languages? Why?
- 40–45: Each records or writes one proverb to "feed the future".
