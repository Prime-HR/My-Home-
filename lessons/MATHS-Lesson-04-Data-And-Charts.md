## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** Election results, fuel prices, your sales — all DATA. Can you read it? 📊
**Say it:** "Don't scroll. Election results, inflation, fuel prices, your own business sales — the whole world now speaks in numbers and charts. The person who can read data isn't fooled, isn't cheated, and makes better decisions. Let's make that you."

## SLIDE 2 — One line
**On-screen:** Data = information stored as numbers. 🔢
**Say it:** "Data simply means information turned into numbers — sales per day, marks per student, rainfall per month. Organise it well and it tells a story."

## SLIDE 3 — The average (mean)
**On-screen:** Add them up, divide by how many. ➗
**Say it:** "The most useful number in everyday data is the average. Add all the values and divide by how many there are. Sold 10, 20, and 30 cedis over three days? That's 60 divided by 3 — an average of 20 a day. Now you can plan."
**🎓 Go deeper (students):** The average is also called the **mean**. Two cousins: the **median** (middle value) and **mode** (most common).

## SLIDE 4 — Charts tell stories fast
**On-screen:** Bar, line, pie — pictures of numbers. 📈🥧
**Say it:** "A chart turns a pile of numbers into a picture you grasp in seconds. A BAR chart compares things. A LINE chart shows change over time. A PIE chart shows shares of a whole. Same data — instantly clearer."

## SLIDE 5 — Don't be fooled by charts
**On-screen:** A chart can lie if you don't check it. ⚠️
**Say it:** "Be careful — charts can mislead. A graph can be drawn to make a tiny change look huge, or hide the real numbers. Always check: what are the labels? Where does the scale start? Who made this, and why? Smart people question the picture."

## SLIDE 6 — Use data in YOUR life
**On-screen:** Track sales, marks, spending → decide better. 🎯
**Say it:** "Remember the spreadsheet lesson? Track your sales or your study marks for two weeks, find the average, draw a simple chart — and you'll SEE where to improve. Decisions based on data beat decisions based on feelings."

## SLIDE 7 — Data is the fuel of AI
**On-screen:** Remember Lesson 2 of AI? AI learns from DATA. 🤖
**Say it:** "Here's the big connection. We learned AI gets smart by studying data. So understanding data isn't just school maths — it's the doorway to the biggest jobs of the future: data analysis, AI, and beyond."

## SLIDE 8 — Why it matters
**On-screen:** Read the numbers, lead the room. 🇬🇭
**Say it:** "In every meeting, market, and ministry, decisions are argued with data. The Ghanaian who can read it confidently has a seat at the table. The one who can't just has to trust whatever they're told."

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Numbers are power — learn to read them. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: data is information as numbers; the average sums up a set; charts show stories but can mislead; and data powers AI. Challenge: track one number in your life for a week — sales, study time, or spending — then find its average."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Average = total ÷ count · 2) Charts clarify (and can mislead) · 3) Data is the fuel of AI.

## Quick Quiz
1. Find the average of 4, 8, and 12. *(8.)*
2. Which chart best shows change over time? *(Line chart.)*
3. Why should you question a chart? *(It can be drawn to mislead — check labels and scale.)*

## Teacher version (45 min)
- 0–5: Show a real chart (e.g., fuel prices); ask what it says.
- 5–18: Teach average and the three chart types.
- 18–30: Students collect class data and find the average.
- 30–40: Draw a simple chart; discuss how charts can mislead.
- 40–45: Each picks one number to track for a week.
