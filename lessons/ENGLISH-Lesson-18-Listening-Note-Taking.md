# Pillar 1 · English Language — Lesson 18: Listening & Note-Taking
### "The best students don't write everything — they capture what matters"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.3 English · **Lesson:** 18 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 + professionals · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Listen actively for key points. 2) Take clear, useful notes. 3) Review notes to remember more.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Two students, same class. One remembers; one forgets. The difference? Notes. 📝
**Say it:** "In the same lesson, one student walks out remembering everything, another remembers almost nothing. The secret weapon is active listening and good note-taking. It's a skill that wins exams and impresses any boss. Let's learn it."

## SLIDE 2 — Active listening
**On-screen:** Listen for the MAIN points, not every word. 👂
**Say it:** "Don't try to catch every single word — you'll fall behind. Listen for the main ideas, the words a teacher repeats or stresses, and signals like 'most importantly' or 'three reasons'. Catch the big bones."

## SLIDE 3 — Don't write everything
**On-screen:** Key words, not full sentences. ✍️
**Say it:** "Good notes are short. Write key words and short phrases, not whole sentences. You can't listen and write a paragraph at once. Capture the point in a few words and keep listening."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Use your own short-forms — arrows, abbreviations, symbols — to write faster."

## SLIDE 4 — Organise as you go
**On-screen:** Headings, bullets, spacing. 📋
**Say it:** "Lay notes out clearly — a heading for each topic, bullet points for ideas, space to add later. Organised notes are easy to revise from. Messy notes are often useless by the next week."

## SLIDE 5 — Review soon after
**On-screen:** Read them within a day to lock them in. 🔁
**Say it:** "Here's the magic step most people skip: read your notes within a day, fill gaps, and tidy them. This moves the learning into long-term memory — like the sleep-and-memory science we learned. Review beats re-reading."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Listen well, note smart, remember more. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: listen for main points and signals; write key words not sentences; organise with headings and bullets; review within a day. Challenge: take notes in your next class using only key words, then review them that evening."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Catch main points · 2) Key words, organised · 3) Review within a day.
**CTA:** @AIForGhana · raufhusein.site

## Quiz
1. Should you write every word? *(No — key words and main points.)* 2. What signals an important point? *(Repetition, stress, phrases like "most importantly".)* 3. When should you review notes? *(Within a day.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
Read a short talk → students note key words only → compare notes → organise with headings → review-that-evening challenge.
