## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** The phone in your hand is free because someone fought for it. 🇬🇭✊
**Say it:** "You scroll freely today because, not long ago, people risked everything so this land could be ours. Do you know their story? You can't build Ghana's future if you don't know how it was won. Let me tell you in 60 seconds."

## SLIDE 2 — One line
**On-screen:** History isn't old news — it's the story that made YOU. 📖
**Say it:** "History is not boring dates. It's the story of how your nation, your freedom, and your identity came to be — and it's still being written, by your generation."

## SLIDE 3 — Mighty kingdoms before colonisation
**On-screen:** The Ashanti Kingdom & great African empires. 👑
**Say it:** "Long before outsiders came, this land had powerful, organised societies. The Ashanti Kingdom had gold, armies, courts, and the Golden Stool as a symbol of unity. Africa had great empires like Mali. We were never a people without history."

## SLIDE 4 — The fight to stay free
**On-screen:** Yaa Asantewaa led when men hesitated. 🔥
**Say it:** "When colonial forces pushed, Nana Yaa Asantewaa, a queen mother, rose and led a war to defend the Golden Stool and her people's dignity. Remember her name — courage has no gender."

## SLIDE 5 — Independence, 1957
**On-screen:** Ghana — first Black African nation to break free. 🎉
**Say it:** "On 6th March 1957, Ghana became the first country in Black Africa to win independence from colonial rule — a spark that lit freedom movements across the whole continent. The world watched Ghana lead."

## SLIDE 6 — Kwame Nkrumah's dream
**On-screen:** "Ghana's independence is meaningless unless linked to Africa." 🌍
**Say it:** "Kwame Nkrumah, our first leader, dreamed not just of a free Ghana but a united Africa standing tall in the world. That Pan-African vision still guides us — and in this AI age, it matters more than ever."

## SLIDE 7 — Democracy: power to the people
**On-screen:** We choose our leaders. That's precious. 🗳️
**Say it:** "Today we settle leadership by votes, not violence. Democracy isn't perfect, but it means the power belongs to the people. Guard it, use it, and demand better with it."

## SLIDE 8 — Why it matters
**On-screen:** Know your roots to grow your future. 🌳
**Say it:** "A tree with deep roots stands in any storm. Knowing our heroes and history gives you pride, identity, and direction. You are the continuation of their dream — now with new tools like technology and AI."

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Stand on giants' shoulders. ✅ Build the next chapter.
**Say it:** "Recap: Ghana had great kingdoms, brave defenders like Yaa Asantewaa, won independence in 1957, and Nkrumah dreamed of a united Africa. Challenge: ask an elder one story about Ghana's past, and share what you learn."
**Recap in 3:** 1) We had great kingdoms · 2) Heroes won our freedom (1957) · 3) The next chapter is yours.

## Quick Quiz
1. In what year did Ghana gain independence? *(1957.)*
2. Who led the war in defence of the Golden Stool? *(Nana Yaa Asantewaa.)*
3. Who was Ghana's first leader and what did he dream of? *(Kwame Nkrumah — a united Africa.)*

## Teacher version (45 min)
- 0–5: "Why are you proud to be Ghanaian?" Collect answers.
- 5–20: Tell the story: kingdoms → resistance → 1957 → Nkrumah.
- 20–32: Group work — each group presents one hero and their lesson for today.
- 32–42: Discuss democracy and the students' role.
- 42–45: Each writes one way they'll serve Ghana.
