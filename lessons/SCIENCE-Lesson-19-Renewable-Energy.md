# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 19: Renewable Energy & Ghana's Power Future
### "The sun shines free on Ghana every day — why are we still in dumsor?"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 19 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Tell renewable from non-renewable energy. 2) Name renewable sources for Ghana. 3) See the jobs in clean energy.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** The sun pours free energy on Ghana daily — yet the lights still go off. ☀️
**Say it:** "Ghana sits under one of the world's strongest suns, by a great river and a long coast — endless free energy. So why dumsor? Understanding energy science shows the way to a brighter, cheaper future."

## SLIDE 2 — Two kinds of energy
**On-screen:** Renewable (never runs out) vs non-renewable (runs out). 🔋
**Say it:** "Energy comes in two kinds. Non-renewable — like petrol and gas — will run out and pollutes. Renewable — like sun and wind — never runs out and is clean. The future belongs to renewable."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Renewable sources are refilled by nature; fossil fuels are finite."

## SLIDE 3 — Solar power
**On-screen:** Panels turn sunlight into electricity. ☀️
**Say it:** "Solar panels turn our abundant sunlight directly into electricity. A few panels can power a home, a school, even a clinic far from the grid. For sunny Ghana, this is a giant opportunity."

## SLIDE 4 — Water and wind
**On-screen:** Hydro (Akosombo) + wind power. 💧🌬️
**Say it:** "We already use water power at the Akosombo Dam — moving water spinning turbines. Wind along the coast can spin turbines too. Nature's movement becomes our electricity."

## SLIDE 5 — Why renewables matter for us
**On-screen:** Cheaper, cleaner, reaches villages. 🏡
**Say it:** "Renewables can reach remote villages without long power lines, cut pollution, and lower bills over time. They make energy fairer — light for every child to study by."

## SLIDE 6 — Green jobs
**On-screen:** Solar installers, engineers, technicians. 💼
**Say it:** "This is also work. Installing and maintaining solar systems, building and running clean power — these are growing careers. The young person who learns energy science today can power Ghana tomorrow."

## SLIDE 7 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Clean power, bright future. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: renewable energy never runs out and is clean; Ghana has sun, water, and wind in plenty; it reaches villages and creates jobs. Challenge: find one solar-powered thing near you and learn how it works."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Renewable = clean & endless · 2) Sun/water/wind suit Ghana · 3) Clean energy = jobs.

## Quiz
1. Difference between renewable and non-renewable? *(Renewable never runs out; non-renewable does.)* 2. Name 2 renewable sources for Ghana. *(Solar, hydro, wind.)* 3. Name one clean-energy job. *(Solar installer, energy engineer/technician.)*

## Teacher (45 min)
"Why dumsor under so much sun?" → teach renewable vs non-renewable → solar/hydro/wind → benefits for villages → discuss green careers.
