# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 52: How Computers Work
### "Everything your phone does is built on just two numbers: 0 and 1"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 52 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Understand the basic parts of a computer. 2) Know what 'binary' means. 3) Connect science with the digital world.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Your phone does millions of sums a second — using only 0s and 1s. 💻
**Say it:** "The device in your hand plays videos, runs apps, and connects the world — yet underneath, it speaks a language of only two symbols: 0 and 1. Let's open the machine and see the science of computing."

## SLIDE 2 — Input, process, output
**On-screen:** It takes data IN, processes it, gives a result OUT. 🔄
**Say it:** "Remember the ICT lesson: every computer takes information in, processes it, and gives a result out. Your phone is a tiny, powerful computer doing this millions of times a second."

## SLIDE 3 — The brain: the processor
**On-screen:** The CPU does the thinking. 🧠
**Say it:** "Inside is the processor, the CPU — the brain that does the calculations. The faster and smarter the processor, the more the device can do. It's electronics doing maths at lightning speed."

## SLIDE 4 — Memory and storage
**On-screen:** Memory works now; storage keeps it for later. 💾
**Say it:** "Memory holds what the computer is working on right now; storage keeps your photos, apps, and files for later. More storage means more you can keep; more memory means more you can do at once."

## SLIDE 5 — Binary: the language of 0 and 1
**On-screen:** Everything is on/off switches. 🔢
**Say it:** "Deep down, a computer is millions of tiny switches that are either ON (1) or OFF (0). All your music, messages, and videos are stored and processed as patterns of these 0s and 1s. That's binary."
**🎓 Go deeper:** This 0/1 system is **binary** — the alphabet of all computers."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Understand the machine that runs your world. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: a computer takes input, processes, and outputs; the CPU is the brain; memory and storage hold information; and it all runs on binary 0s and 1s. Challenge: name the processor, memory, and storage on a phone or computer you use."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Input → process → output · 2) CPU = brain; memory & storage · 3) Runs on binary (0/1).

## Quiz
1. What part is the computer's 'brain'? *(The processor/CPU.)* 2. What two symbols does binary use? *(0 and 1.)* 3. What's the difference between memory and storage? *(Memory = working now; storage = keeps for later.)*
