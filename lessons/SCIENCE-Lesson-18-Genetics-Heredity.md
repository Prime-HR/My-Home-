# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 18: Genetics & Heredity
### "Why you have your mother's eyes — the instructions inside you"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 18 · **Audience:** Everyone 🇬🇭 + JHS/SHS · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Understand heredity. 2) Know what genes and DNA are simply. 3) See genetics in health and the future.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Why do you look like your family? You carry their instructions. 🧬
**Say it:** "People say 'you have your father's nose, your mother's smile'. That's not chance — you literally carry instructions passed down from both parents. Let's open the code of life inside you."

## SLIDE 2 — Heredity
**On-screen:** Traits passed from parents to children. 👨‍👩‍👧
**Say it:** "Heredity is the passing of features from parents to children — skin tone, height, eye shape, even some talents. Half your instructions came from your mother, half from your father."

## SLIDE 3 — Genes & DNA
**On-screen:** DNA = the instruction book; genes = the chapters. 📖
**Say it:** "Inside nearly every cell is DNA — a long chemical instruction book for building you. Sections of it called genes carry specific instructions, like one for eye colour, one for height. You're written in this code."
**🎓 Go deeper:** **DNA** holds the code; a **gene** is a section coding for one trait."

## SLIDE 4 — Mixing makes you unique
**On-screen:** Parents' genes combine in a new way. 🎲
**Say it:** "Because you get a mix from both parents, you're unique — even siblings differ. That's why one child resembles the father, another the mother. Nature shuffles the deck for each person."

## SLIDE 5 — Genetics and health
**On-screen:** Some conditions run in families (e.g., sickle cell). 🩺
**Say it:** "Some health conditions are inherited — sickle cell, for example, is important in Ghana. Knowing your genetic background helps families make informed health choices, like testing before marriage."

## SLIDE 6 — The future: editing life
**On-screen:** Science can now read and edit genes. 🔬
**Say it:** "We can now read the whole code and even edit genes to fight disease. This is one of the biggest sciences of the future — full of promise and big ethical questions for your generation to guide."

## SLIDE 7 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** You're a one-of-a-kind code. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: heredity passes traits from parents; DNA is the instruction book and genes are its sections; mixing makes you unique; genetics matters for health and the future. Challenge: list three traits you inherited and from which parent."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Heredity passes traits · 2) DNA = code, genes = sections · 3) Matters for health & the future.
