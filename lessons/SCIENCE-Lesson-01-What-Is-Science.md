## SLIDE 1 — The hook
**On-screen:** Why does banku ferment? Why does gari last months but bread spoils in days? 🍲
**Say it:** "Don't scroll — these are science questions you live every day. Why does kenkey go sour? Why does gari survive for months while bread spoils in three days? Science is just the answer to the everyday wonders around you. Let's open your eyes."

## SLIDE 2 — One line
**On-screen:** Science = asking "why?" and finding out by observing and testing. 🔍
**Say it:** "Here's all of science in one line: it's the habit of asking 'why does this happen?' and then finding out properly — by watching carefully and testing — instead of just guessing."

## SLIDE 3 — The scientific method
**On-screen:** Observe → Ask → Guess → Test → Conclude. 🔁
**Say it:** "Scientists work like detectives. They OBSERVE something, ASK a question, make a smart GUESS, then TEST it to see if the guess is right, and finally CONCLUDE. You can solve almost any problem this way."
**🎓 Go deeper (students):** The smart guess is a **hypothesis**; the test is an **experiment**.

## SLIDE 4 — Science in your kitchen
**On-screen:** Fermentation, evaporation, heat — all science. 🍳
**Say it:** "Your kitchen is a lab. Banku and pito ferment because of tiny living things called microbes. Fish and gari last because drying removes water that spoilage needs — that's evaporation. Cooking changes food with heat. You are already doing experiments."

## SLIDE 5 — Science in Ghana's land
**On-screen:** Crops, weather, the Volta, the Gulf of Guinea. 🌍
**Say it:** "Science explains why cocoa and yam thrive in our climate, how the rains come, how the Akosombo Dam turns water into electricity. Understanding it lets us use our land and resources wisely."

## SLIDE 6 — Science vs superstition
**On-screen:** Test before you believe. Evidence wins. 🧪
**Say it:** "Important one. When something strange happens, science says: don't just believe the first story — look for evidence and test it. This habit protects you from rumours, fake cures, and fear."

## SLIDE 7 — Your body is science too
**On-screen:** Food, blood, breathing — a living machine. 🫀
**Say it:** "The science of living things explains your own body — why you need balanced food, how blood carries oxygen, why rest and clean water keep you healthy. Knowing your body helps you protect it."

## SLIDE 8 — Why Ghana needs scientists
**On-screen:** Health · farming · energy · climate. 🇬🇭
**Say it:** "Every big challenge we face — disease, food, power, climate — is solved by people who understand science. The next great Ghanaian scientist could be the curious child watching banku ferment. It could be you."

## SLIDE 9 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** You already live science. ✅ Now ask "why?"
**Say it:** "Recap: science is asking why and testing; use observe-ask-guess-test-conclude; it's all around you, from the kitchen to the dam. Challenge: pick one everyday thing today, ask 'why does this happen?', and find out."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Science = ask why + test · 2) Observe→Ask→Guess→Test→Conclude · 3) It's everywhere in Ghana.

## Teacher version (45 min)
- 0–5: Ask "why does kenkey go sour?" Collect guesses.
- 5–18: Teach the scientific method with that example.
- 18–32: Mini-experiment or demo (e.g., observe sugar dissolving / drying).
- 32–42: Students list 5 science moments in their daily life.
- 42–45: Each writes one "why?" question to investigate.
