# Pillar 1 · Integrated Science — Lesson 49: The Skeleton & Muscles
### "206 bones and the muscles that move them — your living scaffolding"

> Paste into **NotebookLM** to generate slides/audio. **Say it** = narration/teleprompter.

**Subject:** 1.2 Integrated Science · **Lesson:** 49 · **Audience:** JHS/SHS 🇬🇭 · **By Rauf Husein · AIForGhana · raufhusein.site**

**Objectives:** 1) Know the jobs of the skeleton. 2) Understand how muscles move you. 3) Keep bones and muscles strong.

## SLIDE 1 — Hook
**On-screen:** Without your skeleton, you'd be a puddle on the floor. 🦴
**Say it:** "Stand up. The only reason you can is a frame of 206 bones holding you, with muscles pulling them like ropes. Your body is a brilliant machine of bone and muscle. Let's look inside the structure that carries you."

## SLIDE 2 — Jobs of the skeleton
**On-screen:** Support · protect · move · make blood. 🛡️
**Say it:** "The skeleton does four jobs: it SUPPORTS your body, PROTECTS organs (the skull guards the brain, ribs the heart and lungs), allows MOVEMENT with muscles, and even MAKES blood cells inside the bones."

## SLIDE 3 — Joints
**On-screen:** Where bones meet and bend. 🔗
**Say it:** "Bones meet at joints, which let you bend and turn — your knees, elbows, and shoulders. Different joints allow different movements, like a hinge on a door or a ball in a socket."

## SLIDE 4 — Muscles move you
**On-screen:** Muscles pull bones — they can't push. 💪
**Say it:** "Muscles work by pulling, never pushing. They attach to bones and contract to move them. That's why they often work in pairs — one pulls your arm up, its partner pulls it back down."
**🎓 Go deeper:** Muscles attach to bones by strong cords called **tendons**."

## SLIDE 5 — Keep them strong
**On-screen:** Exercise · calcium · sunlight (vitamin D). 🥛☀️
**Say it:** "Strong bones and muscles need use and good food: exercise to build them, calcium from foods like milk and small fish, and sunlight which helps make vitamin D. Strong now means mobile for life."

## SLIDE 6 — Recap + challenge
**On-screen:** Honour the frame that carries you. ✅
**Say it:** "Recap: the skeleton supports, protects, moves, and makes blood; joints let bones bend; muscles pull bones in pairs; exercise and calcium keep them strong. Challenge: feel a joint move and notice the muscle pair that works it."
**Recap in 3:** 1) Skeleton: support/protect/move/make blood · 2) Muscles pull (in pairs) · 3) Exercise + calcium.
