## APPROACH STRATEGY

### Method 1: WALK-IN VISIT (Highest Close Rate)

**Best time:** Tuesday–Thursday, 9:00 AM–11:00 AM or 2:00 PM–4:00 PM (avoid Mondays and Fridays)

**Who to ask for:** The Hospital Administrator, CEO, or Owner — NOT the receptionist.

**Step-by-step walk-in script:**

---

**AT THE RECEPTION:**

> "Good morning/afternoon. My name is Rauf Husein. I'm a web developer based here in Kumasi. I've actually already built a professional website for [Hospital Name] as a demonstration — I'd love to show it to the Administrator or person in charge of marketing. It will only take 5 minutes. Is [Admin name] available?"

*If they say the admin is busy:*
> "No problem at all. Can I leave my card and get a time to come back? I just need 5 minutes — I've already done the work, I just need to show them."

---

**WHEN YOU GET IN FRONT OF THE DECISION MAKER:**

> "Thank you for your time. I'm Rauf, I run a tech company here in Kumasi. I noticed [Hospital Name] doesn't have a website yet — and I know how busy you are, so I took the initiative to already build one for you. Let me show you."

*[Open your phone/laptop to the demo site: raufhusein.site/hospital-demos/[slug]/]*

> "This is a professional website specifically for [Hospital Name]. It has your services, contact details, an appointment booking section, a WhatsApp button so patients can reach you instantly, and it's mobile-friendly — most of your patients are on phones."

*[Pause. Let them look.]*

> "Right now, when someone searches for [Hospital Name] on Google — nothing comes up. With this website, you show up, you look professional, and new patients can find you 24/7."

---

**HANDLING THE PRICE QUESTION:**

> "For a hospital your size, I offer the complete setup for **GHS 1,500**. That includes the website, hosting for the first year, WhatsApp integration, Google setup so you appear on maps, and any changes you want before we go live."

*If they push back on price:*
> "I understand. Let me ask — how many new patients a month would this need to bring you to pay for itself? Even two or three, right? That's what the website does. And this is a one-time cost."

*Lowest you go:* **GHS 800** (only if they're a small clinic and genuinely can't afford more)

---

**THE UPSELL (Hospital Management System):**

After they agree on the website, say:

> "By the way — we also build Hospital Management Systems. Think patient records, billing, appointments, staff management — all in one system. A lot of our clients start with just the website, then upgrade when they're ready. I can show you what that looks like when the website is live. The website is just the front door — the system is what runs things inside."

## OBJECTION HANDLING

**"We already have Facebook."**
> "Facebook is great for social, but a website is different — it's where Google sends people when they search. Facebook can't do that. They work together."

**"We don't have money right now."**
> "I understand completely. I can do a payment plan — 50% now and 50% when the site goes live. That way you see the result before fully paying."

**"Let me think about it / discuss with my team."**
> "Of course. Can I schedule a 10-minute call with you and the relevant person for tomorrow or Thursday? That way I can answer any questions directly. What time works?"

**"We don't need a website."**
> "That's fair — some hospitals do fine without one. But your competitors are starting to get online. Once they're there, new patients will find them first. The website protects the patients you already have from leaving."

**"Someone else offered to do it cheaper."**
> "I'd love to see what they offer. A GHS 200 website usually looks like one — and that reflects on your hospital brand. What I build is something patients see and immediately trust. Can I show you examples?"
